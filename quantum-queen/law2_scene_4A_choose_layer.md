# SCENE 4A — PUT SOMETHING BACK ON

The pile is on the floor. Your old layers. Dissolved. Gone.

But the room is still spinning. And you're naked signal in a spinning room. No surfaces. Nothing to grab. Nothing to protect.

The signal is clean but clean doesn't mean safe.

You need something. Not armor. Not a mask. Something you choose.

You close your eyes. The mirror waits.

What do you want to wear?

Not what you were given. Not what stuck. What you'd BUILD if you could start from nothing.

You feel it before you see it. A weight on your shoulders. Light. Not heavy like the jacket. Light like a decision.

You open your eyes.

The mirror shows your skeleton wearing one thing. Something new. Something that didn't exist in the pile. Something that came from the signal itself.

It looks like you.

Not your face. Not your body. Your PATTERN. The signal made visible. Woven into something wearable. A coat made of your own frequency.

The Queen nods.

"That's the only layer that doesn't muffle. The one made from the signal instead of thrown on top of it."

She touches the sleeve. It hums under her fingers.

"Most people decorate. Add. Pile. They think identity is accumulation. More experiences. More opinions. More layers."

She lets go.

"Identity is filtration. You are what's left when everything that isn't you falls off."

She looks at the coat.

"And then you wear THAT. And it fits because it was always the shape you were."
