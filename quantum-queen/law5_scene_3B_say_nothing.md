# SCENE 3B — SAY NOTHING

You let them figure it out.

The kid walks closer. Studies you. Walks to the photograph next to you. Age twenty. Big smile. Confident pose. Back to you. Back to the photo.

"That one looks more like you."

True. The photo has your face. Your build. A version of your smile that's close enough to pass.

"But it's not."

The kid touches the photo. Then reaches toward your frame. Stops before touching you.

"The photo is cold. You're warm."

They pull their hand back.

"Why would someone hang a warm thing next to all these cold things?"

They walk down the gallery. Looking at each one. Comparing. Their brow doing the thing that brows do when a mind is working faster than the mouth.

They come back.

"I think I get it."

You wait.

"They're all trying to be you. The photographs. They're copies. But they're copies of the outside. The shape. The face. The pose. And you're not your outside."

They look at you hard.

"You're the thing that makes the outside move. And they can't copy that because the copies don't move."

Silence.

"Can I ask you something?"

You nod.

"Did someone put you in that frame? Or did you walk in?"

The Queen's voice. Not from behind. From inside the frame. From the wall itself.

"He walked in."

The kid turns. Can't find her.

"That's the difference between a photograph and a portrait. A photograph is taken. A portrait is given. He walked into this frame and said 'this is me.' Not the face. Not the pose. The movement. The warmth. The question."

The kid nods slowly.

"Nobody can copy someone who put themselves in their own frame. Because the frame isn't the art. The choice to step in is the art. And choices can't be reproduced."
