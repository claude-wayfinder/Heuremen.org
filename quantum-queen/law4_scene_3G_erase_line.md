# SCENE 3G — ERASE THE LINE

You press your thumb to the drawing. Right on the line. The dividing line between your half and theirs.

You drag it. Smear it. Erase it.

The pull in your chest SCREAMS.

Not pain. Alarm. Like a fire alarm in your bones. Every nerve says wrong wrong wrong wrong.

The drawing bleeds. Where the line was — ink spreads in both directions. Your half darkens. Their half darkens. Everything mixes. No boundary. No separation.

And the heartbeat —

Gone.

Not stopped. Gone. You can't feel theirs because you can't tell it apart from yours. It's not two heartbeats in one chest anymore. It's one heartbeat that belongs to nobody.

The glass of water cracks. Splits down the middle. Water runs both ways off the table. Two puddles on the floor. Identical. Meaningless.

You can't feel them.

You can't feel YOU.

The room is still white. Still small. Still one chair. But the presence — the warmth, the fear, the curiosity, the pull — 

Nothing.

You erased the line and the line was the only thing that made the connection a connection. Without the distance there's no between. Without between there's no pull. Without pull there's just —

Two puddles on the floor that used to be a glass of water.

The Queen is standing over the cracked glass. Looking down at it.

"Everyone tries this," she says. "First thing. Erase the line. Remove the distance. Become one."

She kneels. Puts one half of the glass against the other. They don't fit anymore. The break isn't clean.

"Entanglement requires two. Two particles. Two rooms. Two heartbeats. Remove the separation and you don't get unity. You get soup."

She drops the halves. They shatter.

"Soup doesn't feel anything."

She looks at you. Not angry. Tired.

"The line wasn't a wall. It was a bridge. Bridges need two sides or they're just roads to nowhere."

She snaps her fingers.

The drawing reappears on the table. Circle. Line. Two halves.

The heartbeat returns. Faint. Shaky. Like it's been crying.

"Try again. And this time — the line stays."
