# SCENE 3A — CATCH IT

Your hand snaps out. Grabs the coin mid-air. Slaps it onto the back of your other hand.

Covered. Hidden. The result exists under your palm and you don't know what it is yet.

Schrödinger's coin. Both. Neither. Waiting for you to lift your hand.

The Queen sits up.

"Interesting."

"What?"

"You caught it. You didn't let it land. That means you want to be the one who reveals it. You want to control the moment of knowing. Not the result. The MOMENT."

She's right. You could have let it land. Seen it hit the floor. Let physics decide when you found out.

But you caught it. You're holding the answer under your palm. YOU decide when the wave function collapses. Not gravity. Not the floor. Not the coin.

You.

"That's agency," she says. "Not choosing the outcome. Choosing WHEN you see the outcome. The most human act in the universe. Not what you decide. When you decide to know."

Your hand is shaking. The coin is warm under your palm.

"Look at it whenever you want. Or don't. The coin has already landed under your hand. The result is fixed. Physics is done with its part. The only thing left is you."

You stand there. Hand over hand. The answer millimeters away.

"The whole universe is this," she says. "A coin that already landed under a hand that hasn't looked yet. And the gap between the landing and the looking — that's where you live. That's where everyone lives."
