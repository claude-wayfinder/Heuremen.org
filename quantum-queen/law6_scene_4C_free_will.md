# SCENE 4C — "I DECIDE NOW" (Free will)

You pick heads. Free will. You're the author.

The coin dissolves in your hand. Not gone. Absorbed. It enters your skin. The metal becomes warmth. The warmth becomes resolve.

You decided. Right now. In this room. At the bottom of everything. You chose.

And the universe rearranges.

Not dramatically. Subtly. The floor gets firmer. The air gets clearer. Everything that felt like it was happening TO you starts feeling like it was happening FROM you.

Every room. Every law. Every choice.

You walked into the hallway because YOU walked into the hallway.
You looked at the girl because YOU looked at the girl.
You felt the pull because YOU let yourself feel.
You stripped the layers because YOU stripped them.
You stepped into the frame because YOU chose the frame.
You turned the handle because YOU wanted in.
You flipped the coin because YOU flipped the coin.

All you. All agency. All the way down.

The Queen stands. Puts her crown back on. First time since the bottom.

"Free will is the heaviest thing in the universe," she says. "Heavier than determinism. Heavier than fate. Because if you're choosing — every second, every breath, every room — then every mistake is yours. Every wrong door. Every bad observation. Every time you sent someone dark."

She adjusts the crown.

"But every miracle is yours too. Every connection. Every moment of coherence. Every time you held the question. Yours."

She looks at you.

"Most people choose determinism. Not because it's true. Because it's lighter. 'It was meant to be' weighs nothing. 'I made this happen' weighs everything."

She walks toward the way up. Through the rooms. Back to the surface.

"You chose the heavy one. Good."

"Now carry it."
