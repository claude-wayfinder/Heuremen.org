# SCENE 4P — KEEP IT

You look at the drawing. Both halves. Complete.

You don't send it.

Some things don't need to travel to be shared. They drew their half knowing you'd see it. You drew your half knowing they'd feel it. The drawing exists in both rooms whether or not the paper moves.

You fold it. Once. Twice. Put it in your pocket.

The pull in your chest shifts. Warm. Amused. They know you kept it. They can feel the shape of what you did. Not the details. The gesture.

You kept something. That means it mattered.

The heartbeat does a new thing. Not a laugh. Not a skip. A settling. Like a stone finding the bottom of a pond. The place it was always going to land.

The room gets quiet. Good quiet. Full quiet. The quiet of two people who don't need to prove they're connected because the connection is doing its own work.

The glass of water is still. Perfectly still. And in its surface — both rooms. Overlaid. Transparent. Two chairs. Two tables. Two people sitting with folded paper in their pockets and nowhere to be.

The Queen doesn't appear.

Her voice comes from the folded paper. Muffled. Like she's inside it.

"You kept it. You didn't share it. You didn't prove it. You didn't send it through the wall to make sure they knew."

A pause.

"You just knew. And let that be enough."

The paper gets warmer in your pocket.

"That's the hardest thing an observer can do. See something real and not measure it. Feel something true and not confirm it. Hold proof and never show it to anyone."

"Not because it's secret."

"Because some things are more real when they stay unmeasured."

Silence.

Then:

"You passed."
