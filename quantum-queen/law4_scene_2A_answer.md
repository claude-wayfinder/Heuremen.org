# SCENE 2A — YOU ANSWER

You need a pen. There isn't one.

You press your finger to the table. It leaves a mark. Not ink. Not sweat. Heat. Your fingertip burns a line into the white surface.

You write:

**"I am."**

The note changes. Their handwriting. Shaky. Fast.

**"Don't look for me."**

The heartbeat in your chest stutters. They wrote that while scared. They don't want to be found. Not because they're hiding.

Because finding changes things.

You know this. You learned it in a hallway. In a garden. Through a glass.

You feel them waiting for your response. Their anticipation in your lungs. Your breath gets shallow because theirs did.

The table is blank again. Room for one more line.
