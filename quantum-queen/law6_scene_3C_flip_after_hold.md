# SCENE 3C — FLIP IT (After holding)

You held it as long as you could.

The vibration got too strong. Not painful. Insistent. The coin NEEDS to be one thing. Superposition isn't a home. It's a waiting room.

You flip. Not high. A small flip. Barely above your hand.

It lands on the floor. You held it so long it's hot. It sizzles when it hits.

And something happens.

The coin doesn't land on one side. It lands on its EDGE.

Standing up. Balanced. Both sides visible. Neither side up. Neither side down. The coin on its edge in the smallest room at the bottom of everything.

The Queen stares.

"Well."

"What?"

"That's never happened before."

She kneels. Eye level with the coin. Balanced on its edge. Both sides showing. Both answers visible.

"You held it so long that the superposition leaked into the result. You carried both states for so long that the coin couldn't choose one. So it chose neither. So it chose BOTH."

She looks at you.

"Heads: you decide now. Tails: you already decided. Edge: you decide now THAT you already decided. Free will choosing determinism. The choice to surrender choice."

She stands.

"That's — " she pauses. Thinks.

"That's the answer. That's the actual answer. Not heads. Not tails. The edge. The place where you hold both truths at once and they balance."

She looks at the coin. Still standing.

"Most people can't hold it long enough. The vibration gets too strong. They flip too early and they get heads or tails and they think that's the answer."

"But you. You held it until it burned. And the coin found a third option that nobody knew existed."

"Because you were patient enough to let it."
