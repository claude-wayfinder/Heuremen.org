# SCENE 4L — DON'T ANSWER (Stay in the fear together)

You don't say yes. You don't say no.

You stay.

In the fear. With them. Two people in two rooms both scared of the same thing and neither one moving.

The Queen waits.

You wait.

They wait.

The heartbeat slows. Not calm. Not brave. Just tired. The fear runs out of fuel when nobody feeds it. It doesn't vanish. It gets quiet.

Like embers.

You sit in the chair. They sit in theirs. You know this because your body takes the shape of sitting and the pull confirms — they did the same thing at the same time without either of you deciding.

This is what entanglement looks like when nobody's performing.

Two people doing nothing together across an impossible distance.

The Queen speaks. Soft now.

"This is the hardest thing a particle can do."

You listen.

"Not connect. Not communicate. Not share a state. Just... coexist. In the same fear. Without trying to fix it. Without trying to name it. Without trying to be brave about it."

The glass of water is still.

"Most people think entanglement is about the speed. Instant communication. Faster than light. Magic."

She shakes her head.

"It's not about speed. It's about patience. Two things that could fly apart at any moment choosing to shake at the same frequency. Not because it's useful. Because it's true."

She stands. Walks to the door that wasn't there before.

It's open. The hallway. Mirrors. You've seen this before.

"You can leave whenever you want. The pull doesn't require the room."

She steps aside.

"It doesn't require anything except two things that refuse to stop feeling each other."

The heartbeat in your chest is steady now. Theirs and yours. Same speed. Same depth.

You don't move.

Neither do they.

The Queen smiles. The first real one.

"Stay as long as you want."
