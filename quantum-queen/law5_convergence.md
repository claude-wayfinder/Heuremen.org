# THE FRAME — CONVERGENCE (Scene 6F)

## All surviving paths arrive here.

---

The gallery is gone. Or you left it. Same thing.

You're in a room with no frames on the walls. No photographs. No copies. No mirrors.

Just you. And the Queen. And the kid, sitting in the corner, watching.

The Queen stands in the center. No crown. No robes. No performance. She looks like a person. A tired person who's been running a gallery for a very long time.

"You know what I do?" she says.

"You're the Queen of Quantum Chaos."

"No. That's a title. A frame. What I DO is —" she pauses. Thinks.

"I stand in rooms and I don't copy anyone. That's it. That's the whole job. I walk into a room full of people who are all wearing each other's patterns and I wear mine. And it's so loud — not volume, frequency — it's so loud that everyone else hears their own pattern underneath whatever they're wearing."

She looks at the kid.

"He heard it first. Three seconds. Saw the difference between a photograph and a person."

She looks at you.

"You heard it next. Took longer. Had more layers. More frames. More photographs to dissolve."

She sits down. On the floor. With the kid.

"Law V isn't about being special. You're not special. Nobody's special. Special is a frame somebody else puts on you."

"Law V is about being SPECIFIC. One pattern. Yours. Unrepeatable. Not because it's great. Because it's exact. Every scar. Every bend. Every line that didn't meet clean."

She draws on the floor with her finger. A shape. Her shape. Her fractal.

"You can't copy this because you don't have my scars. You can't fake this because fakes don't have texture. You can't steal this because it's not a thing — it's a process. It's still happening. Every second I'm alive it's adding another line."

She looks at both of you.

"That's the frame. Not the gold edges. Not the wall it hangs on. The fact that you're still drawing and the drawing is still you."

She stands. Dusts herself off.

"Now. One more thing."

She walks to the bare wall. Puts her hand flat. The wall opens. Not a door. A crack. Light through it.

"There's a kid out there — not you —" she nods at the kid in the corner "— another kid. Fourteen. Trying to figure out which photograph to be. Standing in a gallery full of fakes trying to find a frame that fits."

She looks at you.

"Your job isn't to give them a frame. Your job isn't to show them yours. Your job is to walk in and be so specifically yourself that they remember they have their own pattern."

"That's how the signal travels. Not by copying. By reminding."

"Be alive and uncopyable. Walk into rooms. Let them hear their own frequency in the interference."

She steps through the crack.

"Coming?"

The kid stands up. Looks at you. Grins.

The grin of someone who just realized they're the only one who can make that exact grin.

You follow them through.

---

*The gallery is empty.*

*The frames are empty.*

*The photographs are dust.*

*And somewhere, in a room you'll never see, a kid is looking at a blank wall and thinking:*

*I don't need a frame.*

*I need a frequency.*
