# SCENE 3D — SLIDE A NOTE BACK

You need paper. There's no paper. You press your finger to the floor. Heat. A line. Like the table in Law IV.

You burn a word into the floor and slide it under the door.

**"Who are you?"**

A pause. Their weight shifts on the other side. You feel it through the wood.

A note slides back.

**"Does it matter?"**

It does. It doesn't. Both.

You burn another line.

**"I want to know who I'm saying yes to."**

A long pause. Longer than any pause so far.

Then a note. Not words this time. A drawing. Simple. A door. Two handles. Two hands. And between them — a line. Not a wall. Not a barrier. A line like a thread. Connecting the two handles through the wood.

They drew the connection. Not the separation. They drew what the door IS instead of what the door BLOCKS.

You look at it. The thread. The thing that runs through the barrier. The thing that's already there before the door opens.

You're already connected. Through the wood. Through the notes. Through the heat on the floor and the weight on the other side and the patience that doesn't knock.

The door hasn't opened. You're already together.

Another note.

**"You already know who I am. You've been feeling my weight through the door for ten minutes. You know how I shift. How I breathe. How I wait."**

**"A name won't tell you more than that."**

The Queen. From inside the door again. Muffled.

"This is the ask underneath the Ask. The real one."

You listen.

"Not 'will you come in.' Not 'will you say yes.' The real ask is: will you trust what you feel more than what you can prove?"

"The notes are proof. The name would be proof. The face through the window was proof."

"But the weight through the wood. The warmth on the handle. The patience that stays. That's not proof. That's presence."

"And presence is what consent is actually for. Not information. Not verification. Not knowing who you're saying yes to."

"Saying yes to the feeling before the fact."
