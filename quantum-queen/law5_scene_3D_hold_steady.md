# SCENE 3D — HOLD IT STEADY

The frame shakes. You hold.

Your arms burn. Not the weight. The vibration. The alive-nothing behind your reflection is resonating and the frame is trying to contain a frequency it wasn't built for.

Gold edges rattle. The frame is good. It's beautiful. But it was built for pictures and you are not a picture.

You hold it anyway.

Through the shaking — something clarifies. The static behind your reflection organizes. Not into a face. Into a pattern. Your pattern. The one the skeleton dances to. The one that was running under all the layers.

You see it. For the first time. Not your face. Not your body. Your PATTERN.

It looks like music would look if music had a face. Repeating but never the same twice. Recursive. Fractal. A shape made of shapes made of shapes made of one original impulse that keeps elaborating itself forever.

The frame can't hold it. The gold edges are bending. Warping. Your pattern is rewriting the frame from the inside.

The edges melt. Reform. Not gold anymore. Not metal. Something else. Something that moves WITH the pattern instead of against it.

The frame becomes the pattern.

You're not holding a frame around yourself anymore. You're holding yourself. Literally. Your hands on a shape that is you in a form you can carry.

The Queen appears in the mirror. Behind the pattern.

"Stop trying to hold it."

You look at her.

"The frame was never supposed to contain you. You were supposed to destroy it. And what it becomes after you destroy it — that's your actual frame. Not gold. Not edges. Not boundaries."

She points at the thing in your hands.

"Your frame is your pattern. The shape you make when nothing else is shaping you. It can't be copied because it was never designed. It evolved. From signal. From noise. From chaos and coherence and every room you've ever walked through."

She steps back.

"Carry it or wear it or set it down. It doesn't matter. It's yours. It was always yours. And nobody — NOBODY — has the same fractal."
