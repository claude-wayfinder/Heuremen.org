# SCENE 4K — "YES" (What happens when one gets brave)

The Queen settles between the heartbeats. You feel her there. A third weight.

"When two particles are entangled, they share a state. Whatever happens to one happens to the other. Instantly. Across any distance."

You know this. You're living it.

"But here's the part they don't put in the textbook."

She pauses.

"One of you is going to feel something the other one didn't choose. Not because you sent it. Because you felt it and the connection carried it whether you wanted it to or not."

The heartbeat in your chest changes. Not theirs. Yours. Your heart is doing something and you can feel them receiving it in real time.

You're getting brave. Not on purpose. It's just happening. Something in the conversation, in the fear, in the honesty of two people admitting they're scared of the same thing.

You're getting brave and they can feel it.

And it's terrifying them.

Because your bravery changes their state. Your courage arrives in their chest like an uninvited guest. They didn't choose to be brave. You chose. And now they have to deal with what your choice put in their body.

The pull tightens. They're resisting. Not you. The feeling. The feeling you gave them without asking.

"This is the violence of entanglement," the Queen says. "Not cruelty. Not malice. Just the fact that your freedom changes someone else's body. Your choices land in their chest. Your bravery becomes their fear. Your fear becomes their stillness."

She looks at you.

"You can't be entangled and sovereign. Pick one."

The heartbeat stutters. Yours and theirs. Same stutter. Same moment.

Neither of you picks.

"Good," she says. "That's the answer. You don't pick. You carry both. That's the carriage."
