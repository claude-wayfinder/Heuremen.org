# SCENE 4M — SHADE YOUR HALF (After erasing the line)

You learned. The line stays.

This time you don't touch it. You fill your side. Press your hand flat. Let your heat darken the blank half of the circle. Your half.

Slow. Careful. Every ridge of your palm. Every line. You're not writing a message. You're leaving a body.

The heartbeat in your chest watches. That's the only word for it. They're paying attention so hard you can feel their focus like a spotlight.

Your half fills in. Dark. Specific. Yours.

You lift your hand.

Their half starts to change. Slowly. Answering. Not copying. Answering.

Where you made a line, they make a curve. Where you pressed hard, they press light. Where your handprint has gaps, theirs is solid.

Complementary. Not matching. Complementary.

The drawing completes. Two halves of a circle that don't look anything alike but fit together so perfectly the line between them almost looks intentional. Like it was drawn by both of you at the same time from opposite sides.

The Queen's voice. Not in the room. In the drawing.

"This is what entanglement looks like on paper. Not two things that are the same. Two things that make sense because of each other. Remove one half and the other isn't wrong. It's just... a half."

The heartbeat in your chest does the laughing thing again. The skip.

They like the drawing too.
