# SCENE 3C — YOU STEP INSIDE

The door closes behind you.

No light. None.

Not dark like nighttime. Dark like before.

Before light. Before eyes. Before anyone was here to see anything.

The hum stops.

Silence so thick you hear your own blood. Your pulse in your ears. The creak of your jaw when you swallow.

You are the loudest thing in this room.

Then — light.

A window. Small. Square. On the far wall.

The garden.

Same garden. Same rain falling up. Same flowers pulling into the ground.

Same girl.

She's looking at this door. YOUR door. From inside the garden.

She's been watching this side the whole time.

You were behind the door she couldn't open.

You had the handle. She had the window.

Same glass. Same distance. But you walked in and she couldn't walk out.

She presses her hand to the glass.

Her handprint fogs from her side. Where the fog touches, the dark room gets warmer.

She's trying to reach you the only way she can.

Through what she changes.
