# SCENE 4G — YOU HUM BACK

You let it out.

The melody that was living in your chest. You open your mouth and give it the air.

Your hum meets the room's hum.

They don't harmonize. Not at first. Two frequencies. Close but not the same. They rub against each other. Friction. Almost painful.

You adjust. Or it adjusts. Or you both do.

The frequencies lock.

And the hallway — *shatters*.

Not breaks. Shatters. Every mirror at once. A thousand reflections of you from a thousand moments all breaking at the same time.

Glass everywhere. Falling slow. Too slow. Each shard spinning. And in each shard — a different version of this. A different choice. A different door. A different you.

You see yourself looking at the girl. You see yourself stepping into the dark. You see yourself speaking. Watching. Knocking. Staying silent.

Every path. Every choice. Every version. All at once.

The shards hang in the air. Spinning. A constellation of yous.

And in the center of it all — her.

The Queen.

She's standing where the hallway used to be. Glass orbiting her like planets. Each shard a window into a choice you didn't make.

She's not surprised. She's not impressed.

She's listening.

To you. To the hum. To the resonance you created by matching your own frequency.

"You found it," she says.

"Found what?"

"The note. Your note. The one you came in with. The one the hallway was built to muffle."

She catches a shard. Holds it up. In it, you see yourself in the garden with the girl. Happy. Connected. A path you didn't take.

She drops it. Catches another. You in the dark room. Alone. Quiet. A path you didn't take.

Drops it. Catches another. Another. Another.

"All of these are real," she says. "Every path you didn't take still happened. You just can't see them because you're standing in this one."

She opens her hands. Every shard floats back into place. Not a hallway anymore. A sphere. You're inside it. Every possible version of this moment curving around you.

"That's superposition," she says. "Not a trick. Not a riddle. Just the fact that you contain every version of yourself and the one you experience is the one you hum into being."

She looks at you.

"So. What do you want to hum next?"
