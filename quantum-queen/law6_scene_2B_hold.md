# SCENE 2B — HOLD IT

You close your hand around the coin. Not flipping. Holding.

The metal is warm in your fist. Both sides pressed against your skin at once. Heads touching your palm. Tails touching your fingers. Both sides real. Both sides felt. Neither one chosen.

The Queen looks at your closed fist.

"You're holding superposition in your hand."

You squeeze tighter.

"As long as you don't flip it, both states exist. You ARE deciding and you ALREADY decided. Free will and determinism. Living in the same hand. Right now."

She lies back down.

"Most people flip immediately. Can't stand the not-knowing. Can't hold both possibilities at once. The uncertainty is physically uncomfortable. Like holding your breath."

You feel it. She's right. The coin in your hand is humming. Vibrating between states. It WANTS to be flipped. It wants to collapse into one answer. The uncertainty is pressure.

"But here's the thing nobody tells you about the bottom," she says to the ceiling.

"The bottom isn't the answer. It's not heads or tails. The bottom is this. RIGHT this. The holding. The moment before. The last second of not-knowing."

"Every room you walked through. Every mirror. Every door. Every choice. They all felt important. And they were. But they all sit on top of this — the moment where you hold both sides of the only question and you don't look."

You hold the coin. The vibration is intense now.

"How long can you hold it?"

"As long as you need to."

"What happens if I hold it forever?"

She smiles. At the ceiling.

"Then you live in the question. And the question is the most honest place in the universe. Because it's the only place where both answers are true."
