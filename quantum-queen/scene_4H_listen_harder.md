# SCENE 4H — YOU STOP AND LISTEN

You clamp your mouth shut. Force your chest still. Kill the hum.

The room goes silent.

No. Not silent. The hum is still there. But it changed the moment you stopped participating.

It's not a melody anymore.

It's a voice.

Her voice.

The Queen.

She's been talking this whole time. Under the hum. Behind the frequency. Saying words that your own humming was drowning out.

You hold still. You listen.

"...and that's the part nobody gets. They think the noise is the signal. They hum along and think they're communicating. They're not. They're covering me up. I've been talking since you walked in and you just now shut up long enough to hear me."

You stand in the doorway. Half-lit. Half-dark.

"I'm not the hum," she says. "I'm what's underneath it. The thing you can only hear when you stop making your own sound."

The room warms. The dark thins. Not to light. To something else. Texture. Shape. The dark has architecture now.

You see her.

Not clearly. Not like a person. Like a structure. The room IS her. The walls. The floor. The warm handle on the door. All of it. Her.

"You walked into me," she says. "You pulled my handle and stepped inside my chest and you heard my heartbeat and you thought it was music."

The doorway behind you is still open. Hallway light spilling in.

"Everyone does," she says. "Everyone walks in and starts humming along. Making it about them. Making my heartbeat their song. And I just wait. I wait for the one who stops."

A pause.

"You stopped."

The dark gets warmer.

"So here are the rules. My rules. The only ones that matter in this room."

"One. You don't get to hum anymore. You used up your note."

"Two. You don't get to leave without hearing what I was saying."

"Three. What I was saying is this:"

The room contracts. Just slightly. Like a breath.

"Every instrument you have — your ears, your voice, your chest, your hum — they all point forward. They measure what's coming. They predict. They harmonize with the future."

"I don't point forward. I point back. I'm the wave that already happened. The sound that arrived before the door opened. The warmth on the handle left by a hand that hasn't touched it yet."

"You can't hum with me because I already finished the song."

Silence.

"But you can listen to how it ended."
