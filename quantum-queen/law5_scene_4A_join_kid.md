# SCENE 4A — STEP OUT (Join the kid)

You step out of the frame.

The gold edges ring like a bell when you leave. The frame stays on the wall. Still empty. Still warm where you were.

You stand next to the kid. Both of you looking at it.

"It's still warm," the kid says.

"Yeah."

"Can anyone else step in?"

You look at the frame. Your size. Your shoulders. Your height.

"They could stand in it. But it wouldn't fit."

"Because it's your size?"

"Because it's my shape. Not my body shape. My — " You stop.

"Your question shape," the kid says.

Yeah. That.

The Queen appears at the end of the gallery. Walking toward you. Slow. Looking at the empty frames on the walls. The dissolved photographs.

"You left something in the frame," she says.

You look. The frame isn't empty. There's something in it. Faint. Like heat haze on a highway. A shape made of absence. The outline of where you were.

"That's the part they'll copy," she says. "The outline. The residue. The shape of where you stood. People will walk up to this frame and try to fit themselves into your ghost. They'll pose like you posed. Speak like you spoke. Carry what you carried."

She touches the haze. It ripples.

"And it won't work. Because the ghost is a shell. The shape without the signal. The frame without the wave function."

She looks at the kid.

"You know what makes a hero?"

The kid shakes their head.

"Not what they do. What they leave behind when they walk out of the frame. And the fact that nobody — ever — can stand in that same spot and make it ring the same way."

She points at you.

"He left a shape nobody can fill. That's Law V. The frame isn't the container. The frame is the proof that you were here and nobody else could have been."
