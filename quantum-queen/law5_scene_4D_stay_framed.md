# SCENE 4D — STAY (You chose this)

You stay in the frame. You chose it. You're keeping it.

The kid sits down on the floor in front of you. Legs crossed. Looking up. Like a museum visitor who found the one piece worth sitting with.

Minutes pass. Or hours. Time moves different inside a frame.

The photographs on the walls age. Fade. Yellow at the edges. The copies are degrading. They always do. Copies are entropy in a frame — they look right on day one and wrong by day thirty.

You don't fade. You shift. Your pattern moves. Adjusts. A living thing in a frame does something no photograph can do — it changes while staying itself.

The kid watches this happen.

"You're different than you were when I got here."

"Yes."

"But you're the same."

"Yes."

"How?"

"The pattern changes. The signal doesn't."

They sit with that.

The Queen arrives. Not dramatic. She walks in like she's late for something. Looks at you in the frame. Looks at the kid on the floor.

"How long have you been in there?"

"I don't know."

"Long enough for the copies to rot." She nods at the yellowed photographs.

"Here's the thing about staying in the frame. It's brave. It's real. It's a commitment to being seen exactly as you are."

She pauses.

"But the frame is still a wall. Four sides. A boundary. A decision about where you end."

She kneels next to the kid.

"He's showing you something important. That real doesn't fade. That signal doesn't rot. That you can be the same thing for a long time and still be moving."

She looks at you.

"But he's also showing you something dangerous. That you can choose a frame so perfect it becomes a prison. A golden cage that fits exactly. And the fit is so good you forget you're contained."

She stands.

"Stay if you want. The frame is real. You're real in it. But know what you're trading. Every direction you're not going. Every room you're not walking through. Every signal you're not colliding with."

She touches the gold edge.

"A perfect frame is a beautiful trap. The most comfortable cage in the world is still a cage if you can't leave."
