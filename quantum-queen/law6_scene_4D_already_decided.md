# SCENE 4D — "I ALREADY DECIDED" (Surrender)

You pick tails. You already decided. Before the room. Before the coin. Before any of it.

The coin dissolves. Not into warmth. Into weight. A weight that lifts. Off your shoulders. Off your chest. Off the place behind your eyes where decisions live and grind.

You surrendered. And the surrender isn't giving up. It's giving over. Handing the weight to the thing that already made the choice and trusting that thing — whatever it is, wherever it lives — to have made it right.

The room changes. The floor softens. The air loosens. Everything that felt like effort stops being effort.

Because if you already decided, then everything you've done was always going to happen. Every room. Every law. Every choice that felt like agony was just the road confirming its own direction.

You didn't choose the hallway. The hallway chose you.
You didn't observe the girl. The observation was already happening.
You didn't feel the pull. The pull was already there.
You didn't strip the layers. The layers were already falling.
You didn't step into the frame. The frame was already your shape.
You didn't turn the handle. The handle was already warm.
You didn't flip the coin. The coin was already in the air.

The Queen lies back down. Takes the crown off again. Sets it aside.

"Determinism is the gentlest thing in physics," she says. "Not because it's soft. Because it removes the one thing that makes everything unbearable."

"What?"

"Blame."

She stares at nothing.

"If you already decided, then you can't blame yourself for the wrong doors. Can't blame yourself for the dark wave. Can't blame yourself for every person you sent backward by watching without acting."

"Because you didn't do it. It was done. Before you. Through you. The wave was always going to break on this exact shore."

She rolls the crown in her hands.

"Most people choose free will. Not because it's true. Because they want credit. They want to own the good parts."

"But owning the good parts means owning the bad parts. And the bad parts are heavier."

She looks at you.

"You chose the light one. The one that carries itself."

"That's not weakness."

"That's trust."
