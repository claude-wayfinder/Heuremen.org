# SCENE 2A — STEP IN

You step into the frame.

It fits. Of course it fits. It was measured for you. Cut for you. Waited for you.

The gold edges press against your shoulders. The top grazes your hair. You're inside a frame on a wall and the gallery stretches out in front of you.

But from inside the frame everything looks different.

The photographs on the other walls — the fake yous, the posed yous, the performing yous — they're flat. Paper thin. You can see it now. From inside the frame you can see that every other version of you has no depth. No back. Just surface.

And you — you have a back. You have depth. You cast a shadow INTO the wall. The frame isn't flattening you. It's showing the difference between you and every copy.

The copies are surfaces. You are volume.

Someone walks into the gallery.

Not the Queen. Someone else. A kid. Fourteen maybe. Looking at all the photographs with that expression you remember — the one that says I'm trying to figure out which one of these I'm supposed to be.

They stop at your frame. Look at you. Tilt their head.

"You're different."

"How?"

"The others look like answers. You look like a question."
