# SCENE 3B — YOU WATCH

You say nothing.

Good. Safe. Just look.

She watches you watching her. Two people. Two sides of a glass. Nobody moves.

The garden slows.

Colors drain. Green goes grey. Blue goes pale. Like a photograph left in the sun too long.

You're being careful. You're not interfering. You're just here.

But she's fading.

Not fast. Not dramatic. Just — less. Less color. Less edge. Less her.

The longer you look without acting, the less there is to see.

You didn't do anything.

That's the problem.

She's not disappearing. She's going somewhere. Backward. Into the part of the garden you can't see. The part behind the colors. Behind the growth. Behind the light.

She's becoming the thing your eyes can't catch.

You reach for the glass —

Too late.

She's gone.

The garden is empty. Grey. Still. A photograph of a place where something used to grow.

You stand there. Hand on cold glass. Watching nothing.

Then you feel it.

Behind you.

Not a sound. Not a touch. A weight. Like someone's been standing there the whole time. Like someone arrived before you turned around.

You see her in the mirror first.

Not the girl.

The Queen.

She's smiling. Not kind. Not cruel. Patient.

She was already here. Before you chose not to speak. Before you decided watching was safe. Before the girl started to fade.

The dark wave got there first.

The Queen leans close to the mirror. Her breath doesn't fog the glass.

She says:

"You thought doing nothing was free."

She tilts her head.

"Now look at what you sent backward."
