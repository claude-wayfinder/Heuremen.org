# THE FRAME (Law V — You Can't Copy the Hero)

## Scene 1 — The Gallery

You walk into a room full of you.

Hundreds. On the walls. In frames. Every version of you that ever existed. Age five in a frame. Age twelve. Age fifteen pretending to be twenty. Age twenty pretending to be fine.

Every photograph is perfect. Lit right. Posed right. The version of you that would get the most likes. The version that would make your mother proud. The version that strangers would trust.

None of them are you.

You know this immediately. The way you know a cover band. The notes are right. The timing is right. Everything is right and nothing is real.

You walk through the gallery. Face after face. Year after year. All yours. All wrong.

At the end of the hall — a blank frame. Empty. Gold edges. A small placard underneath.

It says:

**"This one is real. Step inside."**

The frame is your size. Exactly your size. The edges would touch your shoulders. The top would touch your hair.

Two options.

---

> **STEP IN** — become the picture → Scene 2A
> **TAKE THE FRAME OFF THE WALL** — carry it with you → Scene 2B
