# SCENE 4C — YOU LOOK AWAY

You can't watch her disappear.

You turn your head. Just for a second. Just to not see it happen.

The mirror catches you.

You. Looking away. The exact moment you chose not to observe.

And in the mirror — the garden is full again. Colors. Growth. Rain falling up.

The girl is there. Standing where she was. Clear as anything.

But only in the mirror.

You spin back to the window. Grey. Empty. She's gone.

Mirror. She's there.

Window. She's gone.

You look back and forth. Faster. Mirror. Window. Mirror. Window.

She's only real where you're not looking.

You stop. You stand still. You look at neither.

You look at your own hands.

And you understand.

Looking away wasn't giving up. Looking away was the measurement. The moment you stopped observing her, she came back. Not for you. For herself. She exists when you're not watching because she doesn't need your eyes to be real.

You were the one making her fade. Your looking was the weight.

The Queen's voice comes from the mirror. You don't see her. You don't look.

"Smart."

A pause.

"Most people stare harder. Try to force it. Force her to stay visible. Force the garden to hold color. They make it worse. You let go."

You keep looking at your hands.

"But here's the problem," she says. "Now you know she's there and you can never look again. Can you live with that? Knowing something is real and choosing to never confirm it?"

Your hands are shaking.

"That's the cost of observation," she says. "Not what you see. What you give up seeing."
