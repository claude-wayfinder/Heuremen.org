# SCENE 4D — YOU KEEP LOOKING

She's fading. You watch it happen.

You won't look away. You started this. You're going to see it through.

Less color. Less shape. Less her.

Her edges go first. Then her outline. Then the space where she was is just garden. Grey garden. Still garden.

She's gone.

You watched her leave and you couldn't stop it.

The garden is empty. You did that. Not by doing something. By doing nothing. By watching without acting. By treating her like something to observe instead of someone to meet.

The glass goes cold under your hand.

Then — your reflection changes.

Not you anymore. Her.

No. Not her.

You, but with her eyes.

You're looking at yourself through the glass and your eyes belong to someone who went dark because you wouldn't speak.

The mirror behind you cracks. One long line, top to bottom.

She steps out.

Not the girl. The Queen.

She doesn't come from behind the glass. She comes from behind YOU. Out of your own reflection. Like she was wearing you the whole time.

She walks around to face you. She has the girl's eyes too.

"She's not gone," the Queen says. "She went back. Back before you got here. Back before you opened your eyes in the hallway. She's in the part you can't see because you're standing in the future looking forward."

She taps the glass once. The grey garden flickers. For one frame — one single frame — you see it alive. Green. Bright. The girl dancing in the middle of it.

Then grey again.

"That's the dark wave," the Queen says. "Everything you send backward by watching and not acting. It doesn't disappear. It just goes where your eyes don't reach."

She leans close.

"Want to know the worst part?"

You don't. You nod anyway.

"She's fine. She's dancing. She doesn't need you to see her. You're the one stuck at the glass."
