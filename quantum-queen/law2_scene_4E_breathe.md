# SCENE 4E — BREATHE

You breathe.

Not dramatic. Not a gasp. Not a sigh. Just air. In and out. The most basic thing a body does.

The signal in your chest rides the breath. Expands on the inhale. Settles on the exhale. Like it's been waiting for you to do this one simple thing so it could stretch out.

The layers on the ground dissolve. Quietly. No drama. They were already dead weight. The breath just confirmed it.

The room slows. Not stops. Slows. The spin becomes gentle. Almost pleasant. Like being rocked.

The mirror clears. Your skeleton breathes with you. Same rhythm. The thing underneath and the thing on top doing the same thing for the first time.

You breathe and the room breathes and the signal breathes and for one second every moving part of this entire mess is synchronized. Not because you forced it. Because you got out of the way.

The Queen is in the mirror. Breathing with you. Her skeleton visible behind hers.

"You know what nobody talks about?"

Inhale.

"Coherence isn't a state. It's a practice."

Exhale.

"You don't achieve it. You don't earn it. You don't find it and keep it."

Inhale.

"You do it. Over and over. Breath by breath. Every time the noise gets loud you find the signal and you breathe with it."

Exhale.

"That's all. That's the whole law. Find your note. Breathe with it. Let the room spin."

Inhale.

"You'll forget. You'll get buried again. You'll wear masks and carry jackets and listen to voices that aren't yours."

Exhale.

"And then you'll breathe. And it'll be right there. Where you left it. Where it always is."

The mirror is clear. Both skeletons. Both breathing. One room. One spin. One signal finding itself over and over.

"That's coherence. Not a trophy. A breath."
