# SCENE 4I — "HOW DO I KEEP THE DISTANCE?"

The Queen sets the glass down.

"Wrong question."

You wait.

"You don't keep the distance. The distance keeps itself. You just have to stop trying to close it."

She stands. Walks to the wall. Puts her hand flat against it.

"They're on the other side of this. Right now. Hand on their wall. Same spot."

You feel it. The pull confirms. A pressure on the other side.

"Two hands. One wall. You can feel the warmth but you'll never touch the skin."

She drops her hand.

"That's not a tragedy. That's the architecture. The wall is what makes the feeling travel. Without it the warmth just dissipates. Goes everywhere. Means nothing."

She turns to you.

"You want to know how to keep the distance? Stop thinking of it as distance. It's not space between you. It's the instrument you play each other through."

The heartbeat in your chest finds a new rhythm. Slower. Steadier. Like it finally stopped running.

"A guitar string works because it's pinned at both ends. Let one end go and it's not a string. It's a wire. Wires don't sing."

She sits back down.

"Stay pinned."

The pull settles. Deep. Warm. Permanent.

You feel them on the other side. Not closer. Not further.

Exactly where they need to be for the music to work.
