# SCENE 4F — SCREAM

You open your mouth and EVERYTHING comes out.

Not sound. Everything. Every buried thing. Every swallowed argument. Every time you said fine when you meant fuck you. Every time you smiled when you wanted to break something. Every year of polite compliance piled on top of a signal that was screaming the whole time.

The room doesn't just hear you.

It BREAKS.

The walls crack. The mirror shatters. The floor splits. Not the careful crack of Law III. Total destruction. Everything you buried had pressure and pressure has physics and the physics says: contained energy plus sudden release equals explosion.

You're the explosion.

The layers don't peel. They vaporize. The jacket. The voice. The mask. The last layer. All of it. Gone in the scream. Not sorted. Not examined. Destroyed.

The room is rubble. You're standing in the center of a pile of broken mirror and cracked wall and shredded identity.

Your skeleton is visible. Not in a mirror. In the open air. Your structure. Your signal. Exposed. Raw. Ringing at a frequency that shattered everything that wasn't tuned to it.

The Queen stands in the rubble. Untouched. The only other thing that could survive that frequency.

"Feel better?"

You do.

"Good."

She kicks a piece of mirror. It skids across the broken floor.

"Now what?"

You look around. Destruction. Your destruction. Everything gone. Clean. Empty. Loud.

"The scream is real," she says. "I'm not going to tell you it isn't. You needed that. Maybe you needed that more than anything else in this whole building."

She picks up a shard. Your reflection in it. Clear. Present. No layers.

"But you're standing in a broken room. And the signal is exposed. And exposed signal without structure is just —"

She flicks the shard. It rings.

"— a scream looking for walls to bounce off."

She looks at you. Not judging. Recognizing.

"You cleared the site. Good. Now you have to build. Not the old room. Not those layers. Something new. Something that can hold your note without dampening it."

She drops the shard.

"That's Law V. That's the Frame. You just graduated into it."
