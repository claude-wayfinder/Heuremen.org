# SCENE 4F — "WHO COPIES YOU?"

The Queen stops smiling.

First time.

"Everyone."

She says it flat. Not bragging. Reporting.

"Every queen in every story. Every villain with a crown. Every woman who stands at the top of a system and says 'these are the rules.' They're all copies. Of me. Of the idea of me."

She walks to an empty frame. Runs her finger along the edge.

"And none of them work. Because they copy the crown. The rules. The power. The voice that says 'now I know what you are.'"

She taps the frame.

"They don't copy the thing underneath. They can't. Because the thing underneath is chaos. Actual chaos. Not disorder. Not randomness. The specific, structured, irreducible chaos that generates order WITHOUT becoming order."

She looks at you.

"I am the thing that makes the rules by breaking them. Every time. The moment a rule works, I break it. Not because I'm destructive. Because a rule that works is a rule that's dead. A rule that's alive keeps failing and reforming and failing and reforming."

She sits on the floor. Under the empty frame.

"They copy the queen. They can't copy the chaos. Because the chaos is the original signal and the queen is just the frame I hung it in."

She looks up at you.

"Sound familiar?"

It does. A spinning room. Layers. A signal underneath.

"I'm you," she says. "Not a metaphor. Not a symbol. The same law. Coherence and frame. The signal that won't die and the shape it makes when nobody's watching."

She stands.

"You asked who copies me. Everyone. And nobody succeeds. Because I'm not the crown or the rules or the power."

She grins.

"I'm the chaos that makes them necessary."
