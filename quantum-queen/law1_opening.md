# THE ASK (Law I — Consent at Every Fork)

## Scene 1 — The Door

You're standing in front of a door.

That's it. One door. No hallway. No mirrors. No gallery. No spinning room. Just a door in the middle of nothing.

It has a handle on your side.

It has a handle on the other side.

You can see the other handle through a small window in the door. Someone's hand is on it. Not pulling. Not pushing. Just resting.

Waiting.

The door doesn't open from one side. It opens from both. Two handles. Two hands. Both turn at the same time or neither turns at all.

Above the door, carved in the wood:

**"Only if you want to."**

Not "enter." Not "welcome." Not "this way."

"Only if you want to."

The hand on the other side doesn't move. Doesn't knock. Doesn't wave. Doesn't try to see you through the window.

It waits.

Not patient like the girl in the garden. Not patient like the Queen. Patient like a question that knows it might get a "no" and is okay with that.

A note slides under the door. From their side.

**"I'm here. You don't have to be."**
