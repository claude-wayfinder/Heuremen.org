# SCENE 3A — PUSH (Go through)

You push. The door swings toward them. They step back. Make room. Your push didn't surprise them. They were ready.

But they weren't bracing.

There's a difference. Bracing is expecting force. Being ready is expecting arrival. They made room the way you make room for someone you've been waiting for. Not flinching. Welcoming.

You step through.

Their room is identical to yours. Same nothing. Same empty space. Same everything.

Except warm. Warmer than your side. Because they've been here longer. Their presence heated the air. Their waiting filled the space.

They look at you.

"Hi."

"Hi."

That's it. No speech. No explanation. No why-did-you-take-so-long or thank-god-you-came.

Hi. Hi.

Two people in a room that exists because both of them chose to be in it.

The door is behind you. Still open. Handle on your side visible. You could leave. The exit isn't hidden. It isn't locked. It isn't behind a trick or a test or a "are you sure?"

The door stays open because consent isn't a one-time event. It's continuous. Every second you're here is a choice to still be here. The moment it stops being a choice, the door closes.

They sit down. On the floor. Look up at you.

"What do you want to do?"

Not "here's what we're doing." Not "let me show you something." Not "I have a plan."

"What do you want to do?"
