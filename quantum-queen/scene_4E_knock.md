# SCENE 4E — YOU KNOCK

Your knuckle hits the glass.

One knock. Small. Almost polite.

The sound doesn't stay small.

It ripples through the garden like a rock in water. The girl stumbles. Catches herself. Looks at the glass — at you — with wide eyes.

She heard you.

She FELT you.

Your knock did something to her whole world.

A crack runs through the garden. Not the glass. The garden itself. The ground splits. A thin black line from where her feet are to the horizon.

The flowers on one side bloom forward. The flowers on the other side bloom backward. Same garden. Same moment. Two directions now.

You did that. One knock.

She looks at the crack. Looks at you. Not angry. Not scared.

Sad.

She knew this would happen. She put her hand on the glass anyway. She was hoping you wouldn't knock. Hoping you'd just be there. On the other side. Close enough to feel.

But you had to know. You had to confirm. You had to reach through.

The crack widens.

The Queen rises out of it.

Not walks. Rises. Like the crack is a door that goes down instead of sideways. She climbs out of the split in the garden and stands between the two halves.

Forward-blooming flowers on her left. Backward-blooming on her right.

She looks at you through the glass.

"You knocked."

"I had to know if she could hear me."

"She could always hear you. You had to know if you could change her. Different question."

The crack reaches the glass. A single line runs up from the ground through the window between you.

"Connection and observation aren't the same thing," she says. "You were connected the moment you walked into this room. You felt her warmth through the glass. She felt your weight in the dark."

She traces the crack with her finger.

"But you knocked. You sent a signal. And signals carry information. And information changes the system. Always."

She looks at both halves of the garden. Both still growing. Both alive. Just split.

"It's not ruined," she says. "It's *entangled*. Two gardens now. They'll never not know about each other."

She smiles.

"Just like you two."
