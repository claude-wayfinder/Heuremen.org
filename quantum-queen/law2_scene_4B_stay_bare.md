# SCENE 4B — STAY BARE

No layers. No coat. No mask. Nothing.

Just signal.

The room spins. Things try to stick. They slide off. Nothing to grab. You're frictionless. Pure frequency.

It feels like flying.

For about ten seconds.

Then it feels like drowning.

Because signal without surface is just noise nobody can hear. A radio tower with no radio. A song with no air to carry it. You're broadcasting and nothing's receiving because there's nothing to receive WITH.

The mirror shows your skeleton and the skeleton is vibrating so fast it's blurring. The signal is clean but it's homeless. No structure to live in. No shape to hold it.

You're everything and nothing is picking it up.

The Queen watches from the corner. Not worried. Interested.

"Feels amazing, right? For a second?"

You nod. You're flickering.

"Pure signal is a fantasy. Physics doesn't work that way. Light needs a medium. Sound needs air. You need a body. Not a mask. A body."

She walks to the mirror. Puts her hand through it. Reaches into the glass.

She pulls something out. Holds it up.

Your skeleton. Physical. Real. In her hands.

"This isn't a metaphor. This is your frame. Your actual structure. The thing that holds the signal in a shape that other signals can find."

She holds it out to you.

"Coherence isn't the signal. Coherence is the signal IN something. The note needs the string. The string needs the guitar. The guitar needs the hands."

You take it. Your own frame. It fits like breathing.

The flickering stops. You're solid. Still signal. But housed. Carried. Shaped.

"Bare is beautiful," she says. "But bare doesn't survive the spin."
