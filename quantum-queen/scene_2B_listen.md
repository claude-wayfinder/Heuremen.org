# SCENE 2B — YOU LISTENED

The handle is warm.

That's wrong. Metal should be cold. Nobody's touched this door in a long time. But the handle is warm like someone just let go.

You pull.

Dark inside. Not scary dark. Heavy dark. The kind that has weight. You feel it on your skin like humidity.

The hum is everywhere.

Not in your ears. In your ribs. Between your heartbeats. It fills the spaces your pulse leaves behind.

You take one step in. The hallway light cuts a triangle on the floor. Your shadow stretches long and thin into the room.

The hum shifts.

It noticed you.

Not louder. Not softer. It *adjusted*. Like a conversation you walked into the middle of, and the whole room changed the subject because you showed up.

You stand still. Let it settle.

The hum finds you. Matches your breathing. Slows when you slow. Holds when you hold.

You've never heard this sound before.

But your body knows it.

Your chest is humming back and you didn't tell it to.
