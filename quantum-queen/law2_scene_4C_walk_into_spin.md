# SCENE 4C — WALK INTO THE SPIN

You kept one layer. The one that earned its place. The scar-turned-tattoo.

Now you walk into the chaos.

The room is spinning. Layers flying. Opinions and fears and borrowed identities whipping past like leaves in a storm.

You step off center. Into the spin.

Things hit you. A judgment. A memory. A version of yourself you abandoned at fifteen. They smack into you and bounce off. Not because you're strong. Because you're resonating at a frequency they can't match.

The layer you kept vibrates. Your signal vibrates. Same note. Same frequency. They've been tuning each other for years.

A big one comes. Your father's voice. Not the words. The tone. The one that said you were too much or not enough depending on the day. The tone that couldn't decide what to do with you so it did both.

It hits.

You stumble. The layer absorbs it. Rings. Not the father-tone. YOUR tone, with his tone passing through. Changed by the transit. Not the same sound on the other side.

You keep walking.

More layers fly. More noise. More chaos. Each one hits. Each one passes through. You're not blocking them. You're not dodging them.

You're filtering them.

The signal stays clean. Not because nothing touches it. Because everything touches it and the signal knows what to keep and what to let go.

The Queen walks beside you. In the spin. Not affected. She IS the spin.

"THIS is coherence," she shouts over the noise. "Not the quiet room. Not the clean mirror. Not the stripped-down skeleton. THIS."

A layer hits her. She laughs.

"Walking through the shit and sounding like yourself on the other side."

You reach the center of the spin. The eye. Silent. Still.

You stand there. One layer. One signal. Everything else flying around you.

"You didn't survive it," she says. "You played through it. Different thing."
