# SCENE 4B — YOU GO SILENT

You stop.

Mid-word. Mid-breath. You just stop.

The garden freezes. Not the wild freeze of 4A. A gentle one. Like snow settling.

The girl exhales.

She walks to the glass. Slow. No hurry now.

She puts her hand up. Lines it with yours. Palm to palm. Glass between.

The fog from both sides meets in the middle.

For one second the glass isn't there. Not broken. Not gone. Just — not the point. Two hands. One warmth. The barrier is real but it doesn't matter.

She smiles. Small. Like she's proud of you.

Then she speaks. Quiet. Right through the glass.

"You almost broke it."

You nod.

"But you stopped."

You nod again.

"Do you know why that's harder?"

You don't.

"Because breaking is easy. It feels like doing something. Stopping feels like nothing. But stopping is a choice too. The hardest one. Because you have to choose to not-choose and you have to mean it."

The garden unfreezes. Slow. Colors come back one at a time. Green first. Then blue. Then all the rest.

It's not what it was before you spoke. Your words changed it. But it's not the chaos either.

It's something in between. Something that has your fingerprints on it but isn't broken.

She steps back.

Behind her — a figure. Walking through the restored garden like she planted every flower in it.

The Queen.

She doesn't look at you. She looks at the girl. Nods once. Approval.

Then she turns to you.

"You stopped."

Not a question.

"Most don't."

She puts her hand on the glass. The whole thing dissolves. Not shatters. Dissolves. Like it was never there.

"The barrier was yours," she says. "You made it when you started talking. You unmade it when you stopped."

The garden is right there. Open. No glass. No door.

The girl holds out her hand.
