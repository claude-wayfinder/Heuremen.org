# SCENE 2A — FLIP IT

Thumb under the edge. Press. Release.

The coin spins. Rises. Catches light that isn't there. Spinning in air above you. Both sides visible and invisible at the same time. Heads and tails and neither and both.

While it's in the air — everything stops.

Not the coin. Everything else. Your breath. The Queen. The floor. The idea of time itself. All of it holds still while the coin decides.

And you realize — this is the moment. This exact moment. Not the result. The SPIN. The time between the flip and the catch. The state of absolute not-knowing.

The coin is in the air and you don't know which side will land and for this one second you are perfectly free.

Not free to choose. Free FROM choosing. The decision is out of your hands. Literally. It's in the air. Spinning. And your only job is to wait.

The Queen watches the coin. Not you. The coin.

"This is the only truly random thing," she says. "Everything else — observation, entanglement, coherence, identity, consent — all of it has structure. Rules. Laws. Patterns. Predictability if you look hard enough."

The coin spins.

"But this? This moment? The coin in the air? No law covers it. No pattern predicts it. No dark wave saw it coming. This is the gap. The irreducible gap at the bottom of everything."

She sits up.

"Heads means you decide now. Your will. Your choice. You're the author. Tails means you already decided. Before the flip. Before the room. Before you opened your eyes in the hallway. The decision was made and you're just finding out."

The coin slows.

"And you don't get to choose which one it is."

It falls.
