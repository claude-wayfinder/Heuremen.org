# SCENE 4F — KEEP WRITING (The notes are enough)

You don't open the door.

You write another note. Burn it into the floor. Slide it under.

**"Tell me something true."**

A pause. Then their note slides back.

**"I'm scared that if the door opens, the notes stop."**

You read it three times.

They're scared of the same thing the person in Law IV was scared of. That confirmation kills the feeling. That opening the door replaces the warmth-through-wood with the ordinary fact of a face.

You write:

**"What if we never open it?"**

Their note comes fast.

**"Then we live here. In the notes. In the floor. In the space under the door."**

You stare at the crack of light under the door. The space where the notes pass. An inch. Maybe less. The smallest possible opening.

And you realize — that crack IS consent. Not the door. The crack. The tiny space that allows exchange without exposure. The minimum viable opening between two people.

You write:

**"This is enough."**

Their note:

**"This is everything."**

The Queen. From the crack. Her voice coming through the inch of light under the door like she's lying flat on the threshold.

"Two people who chose the notes over the door. Do you know how rare that is?"

You listen.

"Everyone wants the big open. The dramatic swing. The face-to-face. They think consent is about getting to the real thing."

"The notes ARE the real thing."

She's quiet for a moment.

"An inch of opening. Guarded by a door. Voluntary on both sides. An exchange of truths burned into the floor."

"That's not a consolation prize. That's the purest form of the Ask I've ever seen. Two people who consent to exactly this much and not one inch more. Not because they're afraid of more. Because this is the right amount."

A final note from under the door. Not from them.

From her.

**"Consent isn't how far the door opens. It's whether both people agree on the width."**
