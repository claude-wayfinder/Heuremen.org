# SCENE 2B — KEEP

You don't strip. You tighten.

The jacket stays. The voice stays. The mask stays. All of it. You pull it closer. Zip it up. Button it down.

The room spins faster.

More layers land. You take them. A posture that isn't yours. A laugh you borrowed. An ambition someone planted in you before you could say no.

You take it all.

The mirror fights. Your skeleton flickers behind the pile. Trying to be seen. Sending signal through the noise.

You can feel it in there. The real thing. Vibrating. Not angry. Patient. It's been buried before. It can wait.

But the layers are heavy now. Hard to move. Hard to breathe. Every layer is a choice someone else made about who you are and you're carrying all of them.

The room spins.

You stand still.

The layers press in. Not painful. Suffocating. The difference between pain and suffocation is that pain says stop and suffocation just slowly runs out of air.

You're running out of air.

The mirror goes dark. Too many layers. The signal can't get through. Your skeleton vanishes.

And for one horrible second — you don't know what's underneath anymore. Not because it's gone. Because you buried it so deep you can't remember what it looked like.

The room stops spinning.

Everything is still.

Quiet.

Two options.
