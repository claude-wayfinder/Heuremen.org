# SCENE 4C — CLOSE THE DOOR

You reach behind you. Grab the edge of the door. Pull it shut.

Click.

They flinch.

Small. Fast. A flinch they tried to hide. But you saw it. The door closed and their body said NO before their brain could override it.

You see it. You understand immediately.

You just removed their exit.

Not on purpose. Not with malice. You wanted intimacy. You wanted the closeness of a sealed room. Just the two of you. No outside.

But a closed door in a room with no windows is a cage. And the difference between intimacy and captivity is the handle on the inside.

You reach back. Open the door. Wide. Wider than before.

Their shoulders drop. The flinch unflinches. They breathe.

"Sorry," you say.

"Don't be. You wanted it to be just us."

"Yeah."

"Me too. But — "

"But the door has to stay open."

"Not forever. Not always. Just — I need to know it CAN open. I need the option even if I never use it."

The Queen. She's inside now. She walked in during the flinch. She entered without consent and you both missed it.

She sees you see her.

"I came in when the door closed. Notice that? The moment you removed one boundary, I crossed another. That's how it works. Close one exit and something uninvited enters."

She walks to the door. Opens it wider.

"Consent isn't the room. It's the exits. The number of ways out. The handles on every side. The knowledge — the BONE-DEEP knowledge — that leaving is free."

She looks at them.

"Your flinch. That wasn't fear of him. That was fear of the architecture. A room with no exit is a room that doesn't need your consent anymore. And your body knew it before your brain did."

She looks at you.

"You opened it. Fast. That matters more than closing it."

She steps out.

"Everyone closes doors. The people who matter open them back."
