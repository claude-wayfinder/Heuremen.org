# SCENE 2A — TURN THE HANDLE

Your hand finds the handle. Warm. Their hand on the other side has been there long enough to heat the metal through the door.

You turn.

They turn.

Same moment. Same direction. The mechanism clicks. Both handles move and the latch releases.

The door doesn't swing open. It stays where it is. Unlatched. Free to move but not moving.

Because opening is a separate choice from unlocking.

You unlocked it. Together. Now someone has to push. Or pull. Or both. Or neither.

Through the window — their face. First time you've seen it. Not the girl from the garden. Not the kid from the gallery. Someone new. Someone you don't recognize.

They're not smiling. Not frowning. Just there. Present. Looking at you through a window in a door you both unlocked but neither has opened.

They hold up a hand. Not waving. Showing.

Their palm. Open. Empty. Nothing in it. Nothing hidden.

They're showing you they have nothing. No agenda. No plan. No expectation.

Just a hand and an open door and whatever happens next.

A note slides under the door.

**"Your pace. Not mine."**
