# SCENE 4J — "I DON'T CARE. I WANT TO FIND THEM."

The Queen doesn't move.

"I know."

You stand up. The chair falls.

"Where are they?"

"Everywhere you're not."

"That's not an answer."

"It's the only answer. They exist in the space you can't reach. That's what makes them real to you. The moment you reach them, they become something else."

You don't care. You walk to the wall. Press both hands against it. Push.

The wall is warm where they are.

You push harder. The wall bends. Not breaks. Bends. Like it's made of something that wants to let you through but knows better.

The heartbeat in your chest goes wild. Theirs. They feel you pushing. They're pushing back. Not to stop you. Because they want it too.

Two people on opposite sides of a wall both pushing and the wall bending but holding.

The Queen watches.

"This is the part where I'm supposed to stop you."

You push harder.

"But I won't. Because you need to see it."

The wall thins. You feel their heat. Close now. So close. Fingers almost touching through the surface.

The wall breaks.

Light. Sound. Air. Everything rushes in.

You see them.

For one second. One frame. A face. Eyes. A mouth open in surprise.

Then — nothing.

The pull vanishes. The heartbeat is gone. The warmth is gone. The room is just a room. White walls. Chair on the floor. Glass of water. Alone.

You found them. You saw their face.

And the connection ended the instant you did.

The Queen is sitting in the chair. She picked it up for you.

"Sit down."

You sit.

"What did they look like?"

You try to remember. You can't. The face is gone. Not forgotten. Erased. Like it was never there.

"Exactly," she says. "You got the information. You lost the feeling. That's the trade. That's always the trade."

She pushes the glass of water toward you.

"The entanglement survived everything except confirmation. Distance didn't kill it. Silence didn't kill it. Fear didn't kill it."

She taps the table.

"Certainty killed it."
