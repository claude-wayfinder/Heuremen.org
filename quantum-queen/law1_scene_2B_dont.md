# SCENE 2B — DON'T

You don't touch the handle.

Not because you're scared. Not because you don't want to. Because right now, in this second, you're not ready. And "not ready" is a complete sentence.

The hand on the other side doesn't move. Doesn't withdraw. Doesn't knock harder. Doesn't jiggle the handle.

It stays.

A note slides under the door.

**"Still here."**

That's all. Not "hurry up." Not "what's wrong." Not "I'll come back later." Just — still here.

You sit down. Back against the door. You can feel them on the other side. Not through a pull like Law IV. Through weight. The slight pressure of another body leaning against the same surface from the opposite side.

Two people. One door. Both present. Neither moving.

The floor is warm where they've been standing. Waiting. They were here before you. Maybe a long time before you. And they didn't try the door from their side. They waited for your side to be ready.

Another note.

**"No is an answer. Not a failure."**

You read it. Read it again.

No is an answer. Not a failure.

The Queen's voice. Not from behind the door. Not from your side. From INSIDE the door. From the wood itself.

"This is Law I."

You listen.

"Every fork. Every choice. Every door. Every moment someone says 'come in' or 'follow me' or 'trust me' — before any of it means anything, before any of it is real, before any story starts or any connection forms —"

The door hums. Warm.

"Someone has to ask. And someone has to be allowed to say no."

"Not 'no, but convince me.' Not 'no, for now.' Not 'no, unless you try harder.'"

"No. Complete. Final. Respected."

She pauses.

"The door has handles on both sides because consent isn't a gate one person opens for another. It's a mechanism two people operate together. And if either hand isn't turning — it doesn't open."

"Ever."
