# SCENE 3B — PULL (Bring them here)

You pull. The door swings toward you. Opens into your space.

They're standing there. In the doorframe. Not stepping through.

Waiting.

You pulled. That was your consent. But they haven't stepped in. Because YOUR consent to open isn't THEIR consent to enter.

Two separate choices. Two separate handles.

They look at your room. At the nothing. At the space. At you, standing three feet back, holding a door open.

"Can I come in?"

They're asking. They could walk in. The door is open. You pulled it. The invitation is obvious.

But obvious isn't the same as spoken.

"Yes."

They step in. One step. Stop. Check your face. Not for permission. For comfort. Is this okay. Not "did you say yes" but "do you FEEL yes."

You do. They see it. They take another step.

The door stays open behind them. Their exit. Your promise that they can leave. Not spoken. Structural. The open door IS the promise.

They look around.

"It's the same as mine."

"Yeah."

"Same nothing."

"Yeah."

They smile. Small.

"I like that. Two identical rooms. No advantage. No home court. Nobody knows this space better than the other. We're both visitors."

The Queen. From the doorframe. Leaning. Arms crossed. Not coming in.

"Notice where I am."

You both look.

"I'm not inside. You didn't invite me. The door is open and I could walk in and I won't. Because the consent was between you two. Not me. I'm the law, not the participant."

She taps the doorframe.

"Most laws force their way in. Speed limits don't ask. Gravity doesn't ask. Thermodynamics doesn't give a shit."

She grins.

"I ask. That's what makes me first. Before observation. Before entanglement. Before coherence. Before frames. Before the coin flip."

"The Ask."

"Nothing starts without it."
