# SCENE 3B — LEAVE IT

One layer left. You leave it.

Not because you're afraid. Because you earned this one.

It came from somewhere else. Everything does. But you wore it so long it learned your shape. Stretched where you stretch. Bent where you bend. It's not original equipment but it's not a costume either.

It's a scar that became a tattoo. Something that happened to you that you decided to keep.

The mirror flickers. Your skeleton underneath. Visible but filtered. Like looking at a star through one thin cloud.

The Queen's voice. From the corner. She's leaning against the wall. Arms crossed. Watching the room spin around you.

"Interesting choice."

You wait.

"Most people go all the way. Strip everything. Stand there naked and expect applause."

She pushes off the wall. Walks toward you.

"You kept one. Why?"

You don't answer with words. You put your hand on the last layer. On your chest. Where it sits closest.

She sees.

"Because it's where the signal got through. The signal isn't trapped by this layer. It's shaped by it. Like water through a riverbed. The water is the water. But the river is the shape the water learned from the ground."

She touches the layer. Gently.

"Coherence isn't purity. It's not some crystal-clean signal with nothing in the way. It's a signal that knows its own shape. Including the shapes it learned from the things it passed through."

She lets go.

"You're not your skeleton. You're not your layers. You're the signal that figured out how to sound like itself no matter what it's passing through."

She looks at the mirror.

"And right now — you sound exactly like yourself."

The mirror rings. One clear note. Yours. Through the last layer. Not despite it.
