# SCENE 2A — YOU LOOKED

The glass is cold.

Your hand leaves a print. Five fingers. Fog spreads from where you touched.

The girl doesn't flinch. She expected this.

The garden moves faster now. Vines crawl up walls that weren't there before. A tree grows, drops its leaves, grows them back. All in the time it takes you to blink.

She's the only still thing in the whole garden.

No.

She's the only still thing in the whole world.

Everything else is reacting to you. The garden felt you look. It's performing now. Showing off. Rearranging itself because someone's watching.

She just stands there.

She points at the glass. At your handprint.

The fog is spreading. Where it touches, the garden slows down. Your breath on the glass is doing something. Your presence is leaking through.

She shakes her head. Not angry. Patient.

Like she's done this before.

Like she's been the still point for every person who ever pressed their hand to this glass and thought they were just watching.

She mouths two words. You can't hear her.

But you can read her lips.

"You're loud."
