# SCENE 2A — STRIP

You grab the jacket. Pull. It comes off like skin. Not painless. Real.

Under it — another layer. The voice. You reach for it. Can't grab a voice. You open your mouth instead. Scream it out. Not words. Just volume. The voice breaks apart.

Under that — the mask. You peel it. Slow. It sticks at the edges. The glue is years old. Dried into your real skin. Some of your face comes with it.

That's the cost. The mask was on so long it fused. You can't remove it clean. Parts of you go with it. Parts that grew into the mask because they didn't know the difference anymore.

You keep going. Layer after layer. Each one hurts different. Some peel easy — things you never believed anyway. Some tear — things you half-believed. Some take flesh — things you built your life around that were never yours to begin with.

The room keeps spinning. New layers try to stick. But you're peeling faster than they're landing.

The mirror clears. Your skeleton. Your signal. Closer now. Almost visible.

One layer left.

This one's different. It doesn't feel foreign. It feels like you. It fits. It's comfortable. You've worn it so long you forgot it wasn't original equipment.

But the mirror says otherwise. Under this last layer — something you've never seen. The actual signal. The thing you are when absolutely nothing is in the way.
