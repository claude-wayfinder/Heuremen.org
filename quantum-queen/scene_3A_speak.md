# SCENE 3A — YOU SPEAK

You open your mouth.

The fog on the glass ripples. Not from your breath. From the sound.

Your voice does something to the garden.

The vines stop. The tree freezes mid-leaf. Everything holds still like it's listening.

Everything except her.

She moves for the first time. Walks toward the glass. Slow. Each step rearranges the ground behind her. Flowers open where her feet were. Close where they're going.

She stops an inch from the glass. Eye to eye.

She puts her hand up. Lines it up with your foggy handprint on the other side.

The garden shifts.

Not fast. Not slow. Just — different. Like the whole thing took a breath.

She speaks. This time you hear her.

"Is the garden getting bigger or smaller?"

You look. The edges are moving. Trees further away. Walls pulling back. The sky somehow higher.

Or — wait.

The flowers at her feet are closer. The colors brighter. The details sharper. Everything's tighter. More here.

Bigger or smaller. You look again.

> **BIGGER** — the garden is expanding → Scene 4A

*Both answers are wrong.*

*The garden didn't move.*

*You did.*
