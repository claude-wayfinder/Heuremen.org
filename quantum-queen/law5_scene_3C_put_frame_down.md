# SCENE 3C — PUT THE FRAME DOWN

You set it on the floor. Lean it against the wall. Gold edges on white.

The moment you let go, the frame disappears.

Not breaks. Not fades. It was only real while you were holding it. The frame needed a carrier. Without hands it's just an idea. A shape with nothing to shape.

The mirror is still there. Your reflection. The static underneath. The alive-nothing.

You look at yourself without the frame.

No edge. No border. No gold to say "this is where I start and stop." Just — you. Bleeding into the room. Your signal expanding past where a frame would hold it.

The photographs on the walls shiver. Your unframed signal is hitting them. Disrupting them. The copies can't hold their poses when the original is unfixed and pouring out in every direction.

One by one they dissolve. Not dramatically. Quietly. Like ice in warm water. The gallery empties. Frames with nothing in them. Walls with nothing on them.

You did that. Not by destroying them. By being uncontained. An original signal without borders makes copies impossible. They can't imitate what they can't measure. They can't frame what won't hold still.

The Queen is leaning in a doorway you didn't see.

"You know what the no-cloning theorem actually says?"

You turn.

"It doesn't say copies are forbidden. It says perfect copies are impossible. You can make a shitty copy of anything. People do it every day. Steal a style. Borrow a voice. Wear someone else's frequency like a costume."

She nods at the empty frames.

"But a perfect copy requires measuring the original completely. Every variable. Every state. Every possible position at once."

"And that —"

"Destroys the original. The only way to make a perfect copy of you is to stop you. Collapse you. Kill the wave function. And then the copy isn't a copy. It's a photograph of a dead thing."

She smiles.

"So you have two choices. Be alive and uncopyable. Or be dead and reproducible."

She shrugs.

"Easy choice."
