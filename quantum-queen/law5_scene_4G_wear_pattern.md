# SCENE 4G — WEAR IT (Let the pattern become your surface)

You press the pattern against your chest.

It soaks in. Not like water. Like a tattoo. Like something that was always under the skin deciding to come to the surface.

Your skin changes. Not color. Texture. The fractal pattern from the mirror — the shape made of shapes made of shapes — blooms across your arms, your hands, your face. Not decorating. Revealing. The inside becoming the outside.

You look at the mirror.

You look like yourself for the first time.

Not your face. Your face was always there. But now the pattern is visible. The thing people felt when they stood near you — the warmth, the vibration, the half-second of recognition — it's on the surface now. Readable. Visible.

And impossible to copy.

Because the pattern isn't a design. It's a history. Every moment. Every choice. Every layer you wore and stripped and kept and buried. All of it, compressed into a shape on your skin that tells the entire story to anyone who can read it.

Nobody else has this story. Nobody else walked through these rooms. Nobody else wore these layers and stripped these layers and screamed and breathed and buried and dug.

The pattern is a fingerprint of every moment you survived by being yourself instead of someone easier.

The Queen looks at you. At the pattern. She has one too. Hers is different. Sharper. Older. More broken lines. More repaired lines.

"We all have one," she says. "Everyone. Every person. Every particle. A pattern that can't be forged because it wasn't designed. It just happened. One moment at a time."

She traces a line on your arm. Where it intersects another line.

"This one. This is where two choices crossed and you picked the hard one. See how the lines don't meet clean? That's the scar. The place where the pattern bent and didn't break."

She lets go.

"Copies don't have scars. That's how you tell."
