# SCENE 3D — OPEN YOUR HAND (Without flipping)

You don't flip. You just open your hand.

The coin sits in your palm. Not spinning. Not falling. Just sitting. In your hand. Where it's been the whole time.

You look at it.

It's changed.

Both sides — still worn smooth. But now there's a mark. Small. In the center. On both sides. The same mark.

A dot.

One dot on each side. Identical.

Heads and tails are the same.

Not because someone filed them down. Not because they were worn away. Because at the bottom — at the absolute bottom of everything — the two options were never different.

"You decide now" and "you already decided" are the same thing.

Because every decision you make now was built from every decision you already made. And every decision you already made was pointing toward this moment. Forward and backward. Same dot. Same center. Same coin.

The Queen looks at your palm. At the dot.

"You found it without flipping."

"Found what?"

"The thing under the coin flip. The thing under the randomness. The thing under heads and tails."

She touches the dot.

"Identity. The dot that's the same on both sides. The thing that doesn't change no matter which way the coin lands. The signal under the noise. The pattern under the chaos."

She closes your hand around the coin. Gently. Your fist around the dot.

"You didn't need to flip it. You needed to hold it long enough to see that both sides lead to the same place."

"Where?"

"Here. This moment. This hand. This room. This you. Standing at the bottom of everything you've been through and realizing — every room was the same room. Every choice was the same choice."

She lets go of your hand.

"The choice to be here. The choice to keep going. The choice to hold the question instead of demanding the answer."

"That's the coin. That's always been the coin."
