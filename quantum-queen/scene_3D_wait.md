# SCENE 3D — YOU WAIT

Half in. Half out.

One foot in the hallway. One foot in the dark. The light cuts a line across the floor and you stand right on it.

The hum doesn't mind. It keeps going.

But it changes.

Finds a pitch. Climbs. Drops. Climbs again. Like it's tuning itself. Searching for a frequency.

It finds one.

Your frequency.

The hum locks in and your chest vibrates. Not your heartbeat. Something under it. Something older.

It gets louder. Not in the room. In you.

Your throat hums. You didn't tell it to.

Your lips move. You didn't tell them to.

A melody. You don't know it. You've never heard it.

But your body knows every note.

The hum isn't coming from the room.

It's coming from you.

It was always coming from you.

The room was just quiet enough for you to hear it.

You stand in the doorway humming a song you never learned and you realize — this room didn't make a sound. You walked in carrying the music and the silence gave it somewhere to land.
