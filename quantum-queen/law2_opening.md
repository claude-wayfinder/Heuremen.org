# THE COHERENCE (Law II — Character Stays Itself Through Chaos)

## Scene 1 — The Spin

You're standing in a room with one mirror.

It doesn't show your face.

It shows your skeleton. Not bones. Structure. The shape of the thing you are underneath everything you wear and say and pretend.

You look different than you thought.

Simpler. Fewer moving parts. The things you thought were complicated about yourself — just noise. Under the noise is a signal. Clean. Steady. Yours.

You look at the mirror and for the first time in your life you see the thing that doesn't change.

Then the room starts spinning.

Not fast. A slow rotation. Like a record on a turntable. The walls move. The floor tilts. The ceiling bends.

Everything is moving except you and the mirror.

The mirror holds. You hold. The room goes around you.

But here's the thing.

Every time the room completes a rotation, something sticks to you. A layer. A coat. An opinion. A fear. A thing someone said about you once that you never shook off.

First rotation: a jacket you didn't pick. It's on you now. Heavy.

Second rotation: a voice in your head. Not yours. Someone else's. Telling you what you should be.

Third rotation: a face. Over your face. A mask that looks like the person everyone expects.

Fourth rotation. Fifth. Sixth. More layers. More noise. More shit that isn't yours piling up on the thing that is.

The mirror still shows your skeleton. The clean signal underneath.

But you can barely see it now. The layers are thick. The noise is loud. The mask is heavy.

The room keeps spinning.

A door appears. Appears is wrong. It was always there. You just couldn't see it under all the motion.

Two handles.

One says **STRIP**.

One says **KEEP**.
