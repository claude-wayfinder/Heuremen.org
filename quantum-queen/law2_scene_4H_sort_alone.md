# SCENE 4H — SORT ALONE

"I'll do it."

The Queen drops her hand. Nods. Steps back.

She doesn't leave. She sits in the corner. Watches. Not helping. Not judging. Present.

You start.

The first layer is easy. Your mother's fear of being seen. Not yours. Never was. You just caught it like a cold and never got better. Gone.

The second — harder. A belief about yourself that you've held so long it calcified. You're too much. Too intense. Too loud. Too everything.

You hold it up. Turn it over.

The mirror is dark but you don't need it. You can feel the signal. Is this yours?

Too much. Too intense.

The signal hums.

Not "no." Not "get rid of it." The signal says: that's not a layer. That's a volume knob someone else set. The signal IS the intensity. Someone told you the volume was wrong and you believed them and you've been trying to turn yourself down ever since.

You don't discard it. You correct it. Not "too much." Just: MUCH. The "too" was someone else's word. The "much" is yours.

You keep sorting.

A borrowed ambition. Gone.
A real grief. Yours.
A performed toughness. Gone.
An actual stubbornness. Yours. Absolutely yours.
A fear of abandonment. Yours, but you don't want it. You keep it anyway. It's real.

The pile sorts itself once you stop lying about which parts hurt.

The Queen watches from the corner. Says nothing.

When you're done, you have a small pile of real things. Heavy. Dense. Yours. Not pretty. Not curated. Just true.

She stands up. Walks over. Looks at your pile.

"You kept the ugly ones."

"They're mine."

"Yes they are."

She smiles. Small. Real.

"That's coherence. Not the beautiful signal. Not the clean note. The real one. Including the parts that hurt. Including the parts you wish weren't yours."

She touches the pile. It hums. Discordant. Complex. Not simple. Not clean. REAL.

"A clean signal is a dead signal. A living signal has texture. Interference. Harmonics that don't quite match. That's what makes it YOURS and not a copy."

She looks at you.

"Nobody can fake that. Nobody can steal it. Nobody can copy a signal with that many scratches and call it authentic."

"That's Law V. But you're not ready for that yet."

"First — carry these. They're heavy. They're yours. Walk."
