# SCENE 4G — TAKE HER HAND (Let the Queen sort)

You take it. Her hand is cold. Not mean-cold. Mirror-cold. The temperature of something that reflects without absorbing.

She pulls you up. The layers slide off in the motion. Some of them. The easy ones. The ones that were barely holding on anyway.

She starts sorting. Fast. Efficient. No sentiment.

This one — tosses it. Not yours. Never was.

This one — holds it up, looks at you, you shake your head. Gone.

This one — pauses. Sets it aside. "Maybe."

This one — hands it to you. "Yours."

You hold it. It's warm. It hums. Your frequency. Faint but there.

She keeps going. The pile shrinks. Three stacks. Gone. Maybe. Yours.

The gone pile is biggest. Of course it is. Most of what you carry was never yours.

The maybe pile is small. Three things. Four. The ambiguous ones. The layers that could be yours or could be echoes of someone else's signal that happened to harmonize.

The yours pile is tiny. Two things. Maybe three.

She stands back. Looks at the yours pile.

"That's it."

"That's not much."

"It's everything." She picks up one of the yours-layers. Holds it to the light. Your signal glows through it like stained glass. "Most people are carrying forty things and only three are theirs. They spend their whole life maintaining thirty-seven lies to protect three truths."

She puts it back.

"You can carry three things a long way if they're actually yours."

She looks at the maybe pile.

"Those — you'll figure out. Some will migrate to yours. Some will dissolve. Don't rush it. The signal knows. Give it time."

She kicks the gone pile. It scatters.

"And those? Those were always going to leave. You just didn't let them."
