# SCENE 4N — WAIT FOR THEM TO DRAW FIRST

You sit back.

Your turn. Their turn. You chose wrong last time. Erased the line. Made soup.

This time you wait.

The heartbeat in your chest quickens. They felt you decide to wait. They felt the space you made. The room you left for them.

A long pause.

Then their half fills.

Not fast. Not confident. Tentative. One line at a time. Like someone drawing with their eyes closed. Like someone showing you something they've never shown anyone.

A shape forms. Not a pattern. Not a symbol.

A hand. Their hand. Drawn from the inside. Not what it looks like. What it feels like. The pressure in the palm. The tension in the fingers. The warmth at the center.

They drew what it feels like to reach for someone you can't touch.

The pull in your chest is so tight you can't breathe.

Your half is still blank. They're looking at it. You can feel them looking. Waiting. Not demanding. Just — open.

You press your hand to your half. Not to draw. To answer.

Your heat fills the blank space. Not a picture. Not a shape. Just warmth.

Their hand. Your heat.

The drawing is two halves of a circle. One is a hand reaching. The other is the warmth it's reaching for.

The Queen appears in the glass of water. Just her face. Reflected. Looking up at both halves.

She doesn't say anything.

She nods.
