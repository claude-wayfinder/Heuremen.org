# SCENE 3D — WEAR IT

Maybe the layers ARE you now.

You wore your father's anger for twenty years. At some point it stopped being his. You wore your mother's silence for longer. At some point the quiet became your language.

You wore every teacher who said you were too much. Every friend who said you weren't enough. Every mirror that showed you something you didn't recognize and you believed it because mirrors don't lie.

Except they do. They show you backward. Every mirror you've ever looked in showed you reversed. Left is right. Right is left. And you built your whole identity on a reflection that was flipped from the start.

The layers are heavy. The signal is buried. The mirror is dark.

You sit down.

The room stops spinning. Not because you won. Because there's nothing left to spin around. You're not a point anymore. You're a pile. Piles don't have an axis.

Silence.

Then — the mirror cracks.

One line. Horizontal. Right through the middle.

And through the crack — light. Not bright. Warm. The signal. Still in there. Trying.

The Queen crawls through the crack.

Not walks. Not steps. Crawls. On her hands and knees. Through the broken mirror. Into the dark room where you sit buried under everyone else's idea of who you are.

She sits next to you. On the floor. In the pile.

She doesn't tell you to strip. She doesn't tell you to dig. She picks up a layer. Looks at it. Puts it back.

"Some of these are yours now. You're right about that."

She picks up another. Looks. Sets it aside.

"Some of these were never yours and never will be."

Another.

"And some of these —" she holds one up. It's the one that fits best. The one you almost can't tell from your own skin. "— these are the hardest. Because they're close enough to real that throwing them away feels like throwing away yourself."

She sets it in your lap.

"But they're not you. They're what you'd be if the signal stopped. If the hum died. If the skeleton dissolved. They're the costume the absence wears."

She stands. Offers her hand.

"Keep what's yours. I'll help you sort."
