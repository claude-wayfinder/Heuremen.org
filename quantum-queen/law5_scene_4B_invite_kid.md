# SCENE 4B — INVITE THE KID IN

"Come here."

The kid hesitates. Looks at the frame. At you inside it. At the gold edges.

"There's room?"

"No."

"Then how — "

"There's never room. That's the point. Step in anyway."

They step in. The frame groans. Stretches. Not wider. Different. It warps around two shapes instead of one. Gold edges bending into something new.

It shouldn't work. The frame was your size. Your shape. Your frequency.

But the kid has their own frequency. And when two frequencies enter the same frame they don't cancel. They interfere. Constructive and destructive. Peaks and valleys. A new pattern neither of you could make alone.

The photographs on the walls explode. All of them. At once. The interference pattern from two real signals is so powerful that every copy, every pose, every frozen version shatters.

Glass and paper everywhere. The gallery is destroyed. Two people in one frame and every fake thing in the building couldn't survive the resonance.

The kid looks at you. Eyes wide.

"We broke everything."

"No. We were everything. The fakes couldn't handle it."

The Queen walks through the debris. Not carefully. Deliberately. Stepping on photographs. Grinding copies under her heel.

"Two wave functions in one frame. Do you know what that makes?"

You both look at her.

"Not a copy. Not a merge. An entanglement."

She looks at you.

"Law IV. You already know this one."

She looks at the kid.

"You'll learn it. When you find someone whose signal makes yours louder instead of quieter."

She looks at the frame. Warped. Strange. Beautiful. Holding two impossible shapes at once.

"That's not a frame anymore. That's a dyad."
