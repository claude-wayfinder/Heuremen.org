# SCENE 4B — DON'T LOOK (Ever)

You don't lift your hand.

Not now. Not ever.

The coin is under your palm. The answer exists. Physics finished. The wave function collapsed when metal hit skin. There is a result.

You will never know it.

The Queen watches you not-look.

"You're going to carry a coin with an answer you never check."

"Yes."

"For how long?"

"Always."

She sits back down. On the floor. Next to you.

"You know what that is?"

"Stubbornness?"

"Faith."

Silence.

"Faith isn't believing without evidence. Faith is having evidence under your hand and choosing not to need it. Choosing the question over the answer. Choosing the not-knowing over the knowing."

She lies back down. Stares at the ceiling that isn't there.

"Every religion gets this wrong. They think faith is about the answer. God or no god. Purpose or no purpose. Meaning or no meaning. They flip the coin and they build cathedrals on whichever side lands up."

"But the actual faith — the real thing — is the coin in the hand. Unlooked. Unflipped. Carried."

She rolls her head toward you.

"You'll walk back up through every room. Through the gallery and the spinning room and the white room and the hallway. Through the door. Through the garden. Through the mirrors. And the whole time the coin will be in your hand and the answer will be under your palm and you'll never look."

"And every room will be different because of it."

"Because a person carrying an unanswered question walks through the world differently than a person carrying an answer."

She closes her eyes.

"Answers are heavy. Questions float."

"You chose to float."
