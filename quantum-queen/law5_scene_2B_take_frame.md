# SCENE 2B — TAKE THE FRAME OFF THE WALL

You grab the edges. Lift.

It comes off easy. Too easy. Like it was hung with the expectation that someone would take it down.

You hold it. Gold edges. Empty center. Your size.

It's lighter than it looks. Light as a decision.

You turn around. The gallery. Hundreds of framed yous. All wrong. All posed. All copies of a thing that was never captured right because the thing keeps moving.

You hold the empty frame up in front of one of the photographs. Your age-twelve face. Through the empty frame, the photograph dissolves. Not disappears. Shows what was underneath. A kid who didn't know yet. Who hadn't been framed yet. Who was still moving.

You move to the next one. Age fifteen. Hold the frame up. The pose dissolves. Underneath — a kid vibrating. Trying so hard to be still for the camera. Failing. Alive.

Every photograph. The empty frame reveals what the full frame hid. Movement. Signal. The thing that can't be captured because capturing it kills it.

You carry the frame through the gallery like a window that shows the truth behind every picture.

At the end of the hall — a mirror. Full length.

You hold the frame up to the mirror.

Through the empty frame, your reflection dissolves. And underneath —

Nothing.

Not empty-nothing. Alive-nothing. Movement so fast it doesn't register as a shape. Signal so dense it looks like static. You, underneath every image of you, and you can't be framed because you won't hold still.

The frame shakes in your hands.
