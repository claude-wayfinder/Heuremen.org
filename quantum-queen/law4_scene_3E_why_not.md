# SCENE 3E — "WHY NOT?"

You burn the words into the table.

**"Why not?"**

The heartbeat in your chest kicks. Hard. They didn't like that.

A pause. Long. You feel them deciding whether to answer.

Then:

**"Because right now you can feel me. If you find me, you'll see me. And seeing replaces feeling. Every time."**

The pull in your chest tightens. They meant that.

**"Right now I'm everywhere in you. Your chest. Your lungs. Your breath. I'm in all of it because you don't know where I am."**

Another line. Slower this time.

**"Find me and I'm just a person in a room. One place. One shape. One face. Everything else disappears."**

The heartbeat steadies. They're not scared anymore. They're sad.

**"I don't want to be one thing to you. I want to be the thing you feel when you can't explain what you're feeling."**

The table goes blank.

The glass of water is completely still.

You sit with it. The pull in your chest. The presence without a location. The fact that they're more real as a feeling than they could ever be as a face.

Something moves in the corner of the room. A shadow. Not yours.

The Queen.

She's sitting in the chair across from you. She was always sitting there. You just couldn't see her because you were looking at the table.

"They're right, you know."

She picks up the glass of water. Drinks. Sets it down. The water in the glass is still full.

"Entanglement works because there's distance. Collapse the distance and you collapse the entanglement. Two particles in the same place aren't connected. They're just... next to each other."

She traces the burns on the table. Your words. Their words.

"Next to each other is the loneliest thing in physics."
