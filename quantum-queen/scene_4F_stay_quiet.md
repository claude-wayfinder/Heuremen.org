# SCENE 4F — YOU STAY QUIET

She's right there. Hand on the glass. Looking for you in the dark.

You don't move. Don't breathe loud. Don't knock.

She can't see you. The garden is bright. The room is dark. The glass is a mirror on her side. She's looking at her own reflection and doesn't know you're behind it.

You're in the mirror.

You ARE the mirror.

She touches the glass and feels cold. She doesn't feel you. She feels a surface. And behind the surface — nothing, as far as she knows.

But you're here. Inches away. Watching her search for something she can't prove is there.

This is what it's like to be unmeasurable.

Not invisible. Not gone. Unmeasurable. Present in a way no instrument can confirm. Real in a way no observation can reach.

You exist in this room and she will never know.

Unless you choose to be known. Unless you knock or speak or breathe loud enough for the glass to fog.

But you don't.

Because now you understand something.

Being hidden isn't the same as being absent. The dark isn't empty. The silence isn't nothing. You're here and your here-ness is doing something to the room even though she can't detect it.

The glass is warmer where you stand. She'll feel that. She won't know why.

The dark is different because you're in it. She'll sense that. She won't be able to prove it.

You are the dark wave.

You went backward. Through the door. Into the room behind the mirror. Into the place the light doesn't reach. And you're real and you're here and no one will ever measure you.

The Queen doesn't arrive.

The Queen doesn't need to.

You're already in her kingdom.

Her voice comes from everywhere. From the dark itself.

"Now you know what it's like."

A pause.

"To be the thing that's real but can't be seen. To affect everything and confirm nothing. To warm the glass and never get credit."

Another pause.

"Welcome home."
