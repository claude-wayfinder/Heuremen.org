# SCENE 4H — SET IT DOWN AND WALK

You set yourself down.

Sounds wrong. IS wrong. You can't set yourself down. But the pattern — the fractal, the frame, the thing in your hands that IS you — you set it on the floor and you walk away from it.

Three steps.

You feel it behind you. Humming. Your frequency. Coming from a thing on the floor instead of from your chest.

Five steps.

Quieter. The signal fading. Not dying. Fading. Like walking away from a radio. The station's still broadcasting. You're just out of range.

Ten steps.

Silence.

You stop. Turn around.

It's still there. On the floor. Glowing faintly. Your pattern. Your frame. Your uncopyable fractal self.

And you're here. Ten steps away. Empty. No signal. No hum. No frequency.

Just a body.

Is this what it's like? Without the pattern? Without the signal? Without the thing that makes you you?

It's quiet. Peaceful, almost. No noise. No spin. No chaos to filter. Just a body in a room with nothing to broadcast and nothing to receive.

The Queen is sitting next to the pattern. On the floor. Guarding it.

"How's it feel?"

"Quiet."

"Miss it?"

You don't answer. Because the answer is complicated. The quiet is nice. The signal was heavy. Carrying yourself is work. The fractal is dense and old and full of scars and it takes energy to broadcast it.

"Most people never try this," she says. "Never put it down. Never walk away. Never find out what they are without the thing that makes them them."

She picks up the pattern. Holds it. Your signal in her hands.

"You're brave. Or stupid. Hard to tell from here."

She holds it out.

"But here's the thing. You can set it down. You can walk away. You can live in the quiet."

"But the quiet isn't you. The quiet is the absence of you. And absence —"

She waits. You know this one. You learned it in a garden. In a room with mirrors.

"— is measurable."

"The hole you leave when you put yourself down. People feel it. The room gets colder. The air gets thinner. The silence isn't peaceful. It's a siren that says something's missing."

She holds the pattern out further.

"Take yourself back. The weight is the point."
