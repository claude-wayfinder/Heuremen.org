# SCENE 4E — WALK PAST HER

You don't stop. You walk past the Queen. Through the doorway.

She doesn't follow. She doesn't need to. You heard her. Be alive and uncopyable. Easy choice.

The doorway opens into nothing.

Not white nothing. Not dark nothing. Outside nothing. Wind. Sky. Ground that isn't a floor. A world that isn't a room.

The gallery is behind you. Every frame. Every copy. Every golden edge. Behind.

You're outside.

And outside there are no frames. No walls to hang them on. No gallery to organize them. Just people. Walking. Living. Moving. Each one a pattern. Each one a signal. Each one uncopyable.

And nobody's looking at each other like photographs.

They're looking at each other like music.

Not studying. Not comparing. Listening. Catching a note. Losing it. Finding another. The whole world is a room full of signals and none of them match and all of them matter.

You walk. Your signal does its thing. Not louder than anyone else's. Not better. Just yours. Hitting the air at your frequency. Bouncing off buildings and trees and strangers who feel it for a half-second and keep walking.

That half-second. That's the whole thing.

Not fame. Not recognition. Not a frame on a wall.

A half-second where your frequency brushed past someone else's and both of you felt it and neither of you stopped.

The Queen's voice. Far away. From the gallery you left.

"That's enough."

It is.
