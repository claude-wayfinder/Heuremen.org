# SCENE 4A — YOU KEEP TALKING

You can't stop.

Words pour out. Not sentences. Not meaning. Just sound. Your voice has its own plans now.

The garden goes wild.

Trees twist sideways. Flowers explode into colors that don't have names. The ground ripples like water. The sky folds in half and unfolds backwards.

The girl stands in the center of it. Still. Calm. The eye of your storm.

She's not scared. She's waiting for you to hear yourself.

You don't.

You keep going. Louder. The glass cracks. A spiderweb fracture from where your hand was. Your voice is breaking the barrier between you and the garden.

One more word and the glass shatters.

You take a breath.

The garden freezes.

Every vine. Every petal. Every drop of upward rain. Frozen mid-air. A photograph of chaos.

And in the silence — footsteps.

Not from the garden.

From everywhere.

She walks out of the frozen chaos like it's a curtain. Not the girl. Taller. Older. Same eyes.

The Queen of Quantum Chaos.

She steps through the cracked glass like it's not there. Stands in front of you. Brushes a shard off her shoulder.

She looks at the garden. Your mess. Your noise made solid.

"Beautiful," she says. She means it.

"You know what you just did?"

You shake your head.

"You measured everything at once. Every flower. Every vine. Every color. All of it. All at the same time."

She picks up a shard of glass. Holds it up. In the reflection you see the garden before you spoke. Quiet. Green. Simple.

"This is what it was."

She drops it. It shatters into dust.

"You can't get it back."

She smiles.

"But look what you made instead."

The frozen garden is impossible. Wrong. Nothing grows like this. No color works like this. It breaks every rule.

It's the most beautiful thing you've ever seen.

"That's observation," she says. "It doesn't destroy. It doesn't preserve. It *replaces*. You traded what was for what you made. And you can never undo it."

She turns to walk back into the garden.

"Coming?"
