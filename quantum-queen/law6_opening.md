# THE COIN (Law VI — The Irreducible Moment)

## Scene 1 — The Bottom

You fall.

Not from anywhere. Not to anywhere. Just down. Through floors that aren't floors. Through rooms you've been in. The hallway — flash. The white room — flash. The spinning room — flash. The gallery — flash. The door — flash.

Through all of it. Past all of it.

Down to whatever's under everything.

You land.

Softly. Like the bottom was expecting you.

A room. The smallest room. No walls you can see. No ceiling. Just a floor. And on the floor — a coin.

One coin. Old. Worn. Both sides rubbed smooth by a million thumbs.

You can't tell which side is heads. You can't tell which side is tails. The faces are gone. The markings are gone. It's just a circle of metal with two sides that look the same.

You pick it up.

It's warm. The warmth of something that's been held recently. Someone was here before you. Held this coin. Turned it over. Put it back.

The Queen is here. Not standing. Not sitting. Lying on the floor. On her back. Looking at nothing. Like she's been here a long time. Like this is where she lives when nobody's watching.

"You made it to the bottom," she says. Not looking at you.

"What's the coin?"

"The only question that matters."

She rolls onto her side. Looks at you. Her crown is off. It's on the floor next to her. First time you've seen her without it.

"Every law. Every room. Every door and mirror and garden and hum and frame and spin. All of it sits on top of this."

She points at the coin.

"One flip. One moment. The thing that can't be reduced any further."

You turn the coin over. Same on both sides.

"What are the sides?"

"Heads: you make the decision now."

"Tails: you already made it."
