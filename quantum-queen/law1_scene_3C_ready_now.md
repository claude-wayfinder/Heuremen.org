# SCENE 3C — YOU'RE READY NOW

You stand. Turn around. Hand on the handle.

Different now. Not a first impulse. Not excitement. Not pressure. A decision that had time to settle.

You turn the handle. They turn theirs. Click.

But this time the door doesn't just unlatch.

It dissolves.

Not breaks. Not opens. Dissolves. The wood turns to air. The handle turns to warmth. The hinges turn to memory. The entire barrier between you and them — gone. Not violently. Gently. The way fog burns off when the sun gets high enough.

Because consent given slowly dissolves more thoroughly than consent given fast. Fast consent opens doors. Slow consent removes them.

They're right there. No door between you. No window. No handle. No mechanism.

Just two people in one space where there used to be a wall.

They look at where the door was.

"It's gone."

"Yeah."

"I can't close it."

"There's nothing to close."

They look at you. Not scared. Amazed.

"What did we just do?"

The Queen answers. From the space where the door was. Standing in the outline of something that used to separate.

"You waited. Both of you. You sat on opposite sides and you let the 'no' breathe. And when the 'yes' came, it wasn't through a door. It was through the absence of a door."

She traces the air where the frame was.

"Fast consent needs a door. Handles. Mechanisms. Ways in and ways out. Safety equipment."

"Slow consent doesn't need any of it. Because slow consent isn't an event. It's a state. You're not opening a door. You're becoming the kind of people who don't need doors."

She steps aside.

"That takes time. And trust. And sitting in the 'no' without panicking."

She looks at both of you.

"Most people rush. Most people would rather have a fast yes with a door they can slam than a slow yes with no walls at all."

She shrugs.

"Most people build a lot of doors."
