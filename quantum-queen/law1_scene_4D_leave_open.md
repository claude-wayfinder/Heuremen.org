# SCENE 4D — LEAVE IT OPEN

The door stays open. Wind comes through. Not cold. Moving. The air from outside mixing with the air you've warmed by being here.

They notice.

"You left it open."

"Yeah."

"Most people close it."

"I know."

They sit with that. The open door. The moving air. The fact that you could leave any second and you're not leaving and that's worth more than any lock.

"I had a room once," they say. "No doors at all. Beautiful room. Perfect temperature. Everything I needed. Except an exit."

They look at the open door.

"I would have burned it down to leave."

"But you didn't have to."

"Because someone put in a door. Handle on my side. And I never used it. Never once. Just knowing it was there was enough."

You look at the door. Your door. Handle on both sides.

"The door isn't for leaving," you say. "It's for staying. You can only stay if you can leave. Otherwise you're just trapped somewhere comfortable."

The Queen's voice. From outside. Walking past. Not stopping.

"Staying is choosing. Choosing requires options. Options require exits. Exits require doors."

Her footsteps fade.

"A room with no exits has no consent. A room with no consent has no meaning. Two people in a room with no meaning are just bodies sharing air."

Silence. Wind through the door.

"But two people in a room with an open door? Who could leave? Who know they could leave? Who look at the door and look at each other and choose each other over the door?"

Her voice, far away now.

"That's not a room. That's a home."
