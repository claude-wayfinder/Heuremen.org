# SCENE 4B — "STAY"

One word.

They look at you. The word lands different than you expected. Not romantic. Not needy. Not a request.

A declaration. You are staying. Not asking them to stay. Telling them you are.

They smile.

"Okay."

Two people. One room. One open door. Two words total.

Stay. Okay.

That's consent. Not a contract. Not a negotiation. Not terms and conditions. Two words that mean: I choose this and you choose this and neither of us is making the other.

The room changes. Not dramatically. It gets warmer. The nothing gets more specific. Not furniture. Not decoration. Texture. The air has texture now. The floor has weight. The nothing becomes something because two people chose to be in it.

Presence makes rooms real.

The Queen speaks from outside. Still not entering.

"You know what 'stay' means in physics?"

You look at her.

"A particle that stays is a particle that didn't decay. Didn't split. Didn't fly apart. It maintained its state against every force that should have broken it down."

She looks at the room. At the texture forming. At the something growing from the nothing.

"Staying isn't passive. In physics, staying is the most active thing a particle does. Every microsecond it doesn't decay is a microsecond it chose its current state over every other possible state."

She looks at you.

"You said 'stay' like it was a small word. It's the biggest word in physics. It means: I am choosing this configuration. Right now. Again. And again. And again. Against entropy. Against decay. Against every force in the universe that wants me to be something else somewhere else."

She leans in through the doorframe.

"Stay is a verb. Not a state. You have to keep doing it."
