# SCENE 4A — LOOK

You lift your hand.

The coin is there. On the back of your hand. Result visible.

It's —

You stare.

It's your face.

Not heads. Not tails. Your face. Pressed into the metal. Not a photograph. Not a copy. Your pattern. The fractal from Law V. The signal from Law II. Your face made of your own history stamped into a coin at the bottom of everything.

The other side — you already know. You flip it.

Their face. The person behind the door. The weight on the other side. The handwriting on the notes under the door.

Heads: your face. You decide now.
Tails: their face. You already decided — when you chose to connect. When you turned the handle. When you felt the pull. The decision was made the moment you let someone else into the coin.

The Queen looks at both sides.

"The coin was never random."

"What?"

"It was always going to be this. You on one side. Them on the other. The flip doesn't choose between you and them. The flip chooses which one of you is making this moment happen."

She takes the coin. Holds it up.

"Heads: you're here because you chose to be. Your will. Your decision. You walked through every room and every law and you're standing at the bottom because you wanted to get here."

She flips it.

"Tails: you're here because they chose you. Before the hallway. Before the mirrors. Before you opened your eyes. Someone on the other side of a door decided to wait for you. And their decision pulled you here."

It lands. She doesn't look.

"Both true. At the same time. You chose AND you were chosen. Free will AND determinism. Same coin."

She puts it in your hand.

"Keep it."
