# SCENE 4E — OPEN THE DOOR (You know enough)

You turn the handle. They turn theirs. The door opens.

Not like before. Not a push or a pull. It swings both ways at once. A door that opens in both directions because both people decided at the same time.

You step through. They step through. You pass each other in the doorframe. For one second you're both in the threshold. Both between rooms. Both in neither place.

Then you're in their room. They're in yours.

You turn around. They turn around. You're looking at each other through the doorway. Each standing in the other's space.

You laugh.

They laugh.

You didn't enter their room. You traded rooms. The door wasn't a portal into the same space. It was a swap. You're where they were. They're where you were. Standing in each other's warmth. Surrounded by each other's nothing.

The notes are on the floor. Their notes in your room now. Your notes in theirs. Your handwriting under their feet. Their handwriting under yours.

The Queen. From the door itself. She IS the threshold.

"That's the Ask nobody expects."

You both look.

"You thought opening the door meant coming together. Getting closer. Merging."

She spreads her arms. One hand in each room.

"But the Ask isn't about merging. It's about exchange. I give you my space. You give me yours. We hold each other's empty rooms and we trust the other person to be careful with the nothing we left behind."

She drops her arms.

"Consent isn't 'come into my world.' Consent is 'here's my world, I'll take yours, let's see if we can carry each other's silence without filling it with our own noise.'"

She steps out of the doorway. Gone.

You're in their room. They're in yours. The door is open between you.

Neither of you crosses back.

Neither of you needs to.
