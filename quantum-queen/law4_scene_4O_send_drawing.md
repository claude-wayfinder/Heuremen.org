# SCENE 4O — SEND THEM THE DRAWING

You pick up the drawing. Both halves. Your handprint and their constellation.

You hold it against the wall.

Push.

The paper passes through. Not like breaking. Like mail. Like the wall has a slot you couldn't see until you had something worth sending.

The pull in your chest holds its breath.

You feel them receive it. The moment their hands touch the paper. The jolt. The recognition. They're seeing your handprint for the first time. Seeing their own marks next to yours.

And then — something you didn't expect.

Grief.

It hits your chest like ice water. Their grief. Not sad. Not loss. The grief of seeing something beautiful that you can't hold onto. The grief of receiving proof that someone is real on the other side of a wall you'll never get past.

The drawing made it real. Before it was a feeling. Now it's evidence.

Evidence has weight.

The note appears on the table. Their writing. Shaking.

**"It's beautiful. I wish you hadn't sent it."**

You feel why. The feeling was infinite. It could be anything. It could mean anything. It was potential.

The drawing is specific. It's this. Just this. A handprint and a constellation. Fixed. Finite. One moment captured and now it can never be any other moment.

You measured the feeling. And measurement replaces.

The Queen. From behind. From the hallway you didn't know was there.

"You're learning Law III inside Law IV. Good."

She picks up the note. Reads it.

"They're right. The drawing is beautiful and you shouldn't have sent it. And you had to send it. Both true. Same time."

She sets it down.

"That's the carriage. It carries what can't be sent. You sent it anyway. And now you both know what you lost by knowing."
