# THE CARRIAGE (Law IV — Connection Across Impossible Distance)

## Scene 1 — The Tug

You feel it before you open your eyes.

A pull. In your chest. Left of center. Not pain. Not pressure.

Someone else's heartbeat.

Not yours. Wrong rhythm. Wrong speed. But inside you. Sharing the space between your ribs like a roommate you never invited.

You open your eyes.

A room. Small. White. No doors. No windows. A chair. A table. A glass of water that isn't yours.

The heartbeat in your chest speeds up. Not because you're scared.

Because THEY are.

Whoever's in your chest — wherever they are — they just felt you wake up.

You feel their fear land in your body like a dropped stone.

Then it passes. They calmed down. They're okay.

You didn't do anything. They calmed themselves. But you felt every second of it.

The glass of water ripples. Not from you. From somewhere else. Someone else picked up their glass and the vibration traveled here.

There is another room. You can't see it. Can't hear it. Can't find it.

But it's inside your chest and it has been since before you woke up.

Something appears on the table. A note. Two words.

**"Are you?"**

Not "are you okay." Not "are you there."

Just: are you?
