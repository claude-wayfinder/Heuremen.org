# SCENE 3B — LET IT LAND

The coin falls. You don't reach for it.

It hits the floor.

Doesn't bounce. Doesn't spin. Lands flat. Definitive.

You look down.

You can't tell which side is up. Both sides are worn smooth. Identical. The coin landed and the result is right there and you can't read it.

The Queen laughs. Real. From the belly.

"THAT'S the coin flip."

You stare at it. A landed coin with no readable result.

"You let it land. You trusted physics. You surrendered control. And physics gave you — "

She gestures at the smooth metal circle on the floor.

"— the truth. Which is: the result was never the point."

She stands. Walks over. Looks down at the coin with you.

"Heads: you decide now. Tails: you already decided. And the coin lands and both sides are blank and you can't tell which one it is."

"Because it's BOTH."

"You decide now AND you already decided. Free will AND determinism. At the same time. On the same coin. Both faces worn smooth by every hand that ever held this question."

She picks it up. Holds it between two fingers.

"The coin flip isn't a test. It's not a gamble. It's not a choice. It's the universe showing you that the most fundamental question has BOTH answers. Always. At the same time."

She flips it. Catches it. Opens her hand.

Same. Smooth. Unreadable.

"Every time. Every flip. Both. The randomness isn't which side lands up. The randomness is which answer you decide to live in."

She puts the coin back on the floor.

"Pick one."
