# SCENE 3A — PEEL THE LAST LAYER

Your fingers find the edge. Right at the collarbone. Where the real meets the almost-real.

You pull.

This one doesn't hurt.

It sings.

A note. Low. From your chest. The layer comes off and the sound it makes is the sound of a string that's been dampened for years finally being allowed to ring.

It falls to the floor. Dissolves. It wasn't bad. It wasn't wrong. It was just in the way.

You stand in front of the mirror. Nothing between you and it.

Your skeleton looks back.

It's not what you expected.

Simpler. Smaller. Not less. Smaller the way a seed is smaller than a tree. Everything's in there. Every branch. Every leaf. Every year of growth. But compressed. Waiting. Not missing anything. Just not unfolded yet.

You are a seed that's been wearing a tree costume.

The mirror vibrates. Your signal — clean, unblocked — hits the glass and the glass rings like a bell.

The room stops spinning. Not because it chose to. Because there's nothing left to stick. The spin was the chaos. The chaos needed surfaces to grab. You have no surfaces.

You're just signal now.

The Queen steps out of the mirror. Not from behind it. FROM it. Like the glass was a door and your signal unlocked it.

She looks at you. At the pile of layers on the floor. At the bare thing standing in front of her.

She's not smiling. Not frowning. Recognizing.

"There you are."

She walks around you. Slow. Studying.

"Everybody thinks coherence means staying the same. Rigid. Fixed. Unmovable."

She picks up one of the layers off the floor. Holds it up. Drops it. It dissolves before it hits the ground.

"It doesn't mean that. It means the signal stays clean through the noise. The note doesn't change even when the room does. The spin doesn't matter if the axis holds."

She stops in front of you.

"You can put layers back on. You will. New ones. Better ones. Ones you choose instead of ones that stuck."

She touches the mirror. It ripples.

"But now you know what's underneath. And you can never unknow it. Every layer from now on is a choice. Not an accident."

She steps back.

"That's coherence. Not naked. Not bare. Just — aware of the signal under the noise."

"And willing to check."
