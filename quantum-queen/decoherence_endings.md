# DECOHERENCE ENDINGS

## Where these slot in:

Any binary choice in the tree can secretly be a trap. The reader thinks they're advancing. The Queen thinks otherwise.

---

## DECOHERENCE TYPE 1 — "Both"

*Triggers when: the reader tries to look AND listen. Any scene where they hesitate too long, try to game it, or refuse the binary.*

You look at the left door. Then the right door. Left. Right.

You take a step toward the left.

Then stop.

Turn toward the right.

Stop.

You want both. You want to look AND listen. See the girl AND hear the hum. You want all the information and none of the cost.

The mirrors flash.

Every reflection of you freezes. Not you. Them.

The Queen's voice. Not from anywhere. From the glass itself.

"You didn't choose."

The mirrors go dark. One by one. Your reflections vanish. Not fading. Erasing. Like they were never there.

"Choosing is measurement. You refused to measure. You refused to collapse the wave. You wanted to stay in superposition."

The hallway gets shorter. The doors get closer. The walls press in.

"Superposition isn't a privilege. It's a waiting room. And you don't get to live there."

The last mirror goes dark. You look down. Your hands are transparent.

"You didn't choose what to observe. So nothing observed you back."

Your feet are gone. Your legs are going.

"You don't die here. That would be something. That would be a state."

Your chest. Your arms.

"You decohere."

Your face.

"You were never here."

The hallway is empty. It was always empty.

## DECOHERENCE TYPE 2 — "Cheat"

*Triggers when: the reader tries to go backward. Return to a previous scene. Undo a choice.*

You turn around.

There was a door behind you. You're sure of it. You came from somewhere.

You walk back. One step. Two.

The hallway stretches. The door you came through pulls away. Faster than you walk.

You run.

It's faster.

The Queen laughs. Not cruel. Delighted.

"Oh, this one wants to un-measure."

You stop running. The hallway keeps stretching.

"You opened your eyes. That was the first observation. You can't un-open them. You can't un-see the mirrors. You can't un-choose the choice you already made."

The hallway is a mile long now. The door you came through is a dot.

"Rewinding isn't a direction. Time doesn't go backward for observers. Only for the dark wave. And sweetheart —"

She's right behind you. Breath on your neck.

"You're not dark enough."

The hallway collapses. Not forward. Not backward. Inward. Like a telescope closing.

You fold. Not painfully. Neatly. Like a letter being put back in its envelope.

"Back you go. To before you opened your eyes. To before you were an observer. To the last moment you didn't exist."

Dark.

"Try again. And this time — pick one."

## DECOHERENCE TYPE 3 — "Smart"

*Triggers when: the reader asks about the rules instead of playing. Tries to understand the system before engaging with it.*

You stop.

You look at the doors. You look at the mirrors. You look at the hallway.

You think: there's a pattern here. If I figure out the pattern before I choose, I win.

The mirrors ripple.

"Ah." The Queen. Amused. "A scientist."

The mirrors rearrange. They show you the pattern. All of it. The tree. The branches. Every choice. Every outcome. The garden. The hum. The girl. The dark room. All of it laid out like a map.

"There you go. The whole system. Every variable. Every state. Every path."

You study it. It makes sense. You can see which choices lead where. You can optimize. You can pick the best path.

"Got it?" she asks.

You nod.

The map vanishes.

The hallway is gone.

You're standing in white. Blank. Infinite. Nothing.

"You measured the system," she says. "All of it. Every branch. Every possibility. You observed the entire wave function at once."

White in every direction.

"It collapsed."

Nothing. Nowhere. No doors. No mirrors. No garden. No hum.

"Not into one path. Into no path. Because you didn't choose an experience. You chose information. And information without experience is just —"

She gestures at the white.

"— nothing."

"You understood everything and felt nothing. Congratulations. You're correct and you're empty."

A long silence.

"Want to try again? This time, don't think. Walk."

## DECOHERENCE TYPE 4 — "Bored"

*Triggers when: the reader clicks too fast. Doesn't read. Skips description. Speed-runs.*

The Queen appears in the first mirror. Arms crossed.

"You're not reading."

She taps the glass.

"You're clicking. You're skipping. You're rushing to the end. You want the answer without the hallway."

She steps out of the mirror. Stands in front of both doors. Blocks them.

"There is no end. There's only what you notice on the way. And you're noticing nothing."

She holds up her hand. Counts on her fingers.

"The mirrors showed you the past. You didn't look."

One finger.

"The glass was cold. You didn't feel it."

Two.

"The hum was in your chest. You didn't listen."

Three.

"The girl was you. You didn't care."

Four.

She puts her hand down.

"You want the plot. There is no plot. There is only observation. And you're refusing to do it."

The hallway dims.

"So I'll make it easy."

Dark.

"Nothing to skip if there's nothing to see."

Darker.

"Come back when you want to be here."

Black.

## DECOHERENCE TYPE 5 — "Certain"

*Triggers when: the reader is too confident. Picks without hesitation. Doesn't feel the weight.*

You grab the handle. No pause. No thought.

The Queen's hand lands on yours.

Cold.

"That was fast."

You look at her. She's in the mirror. And next to you. And behind you. Everywhere.

"You weren't scared. You weren't curious. You weren't uncertain. You just... picked."

She squeezes your hand on the handle.

"Certainty is the most violent form of observation. You didn't choose this door. You *eliminated* the other one. You killed it. Made it impossible. Collapsed an entire branch of reality because you couldn't be bothered to feel the weight of what you were throwing away."

The other door vanishes. Not closes. Vanishes. The wall is smooth where it was. No evidence it ever existed.

"Feel that? That absence? That's the garden you'll never see. The hum you'll never hear. The version of you that would have existed if you'd hesitated for one second."

She lets go of your hand.

"Go ahead. Open it. You chose so fast, it must be important."

You pull the handle.

Empty room. Bare walls. No hum. No garden. No girl.

"Certainty collapses the wave function into the least interesting state. Every time."

She's gone.

You're alone in an empty room you chose without thinking.

"Try again. Slower."
