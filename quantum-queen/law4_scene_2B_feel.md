# SCENE 2B — YOU FEEL

You don't write. Writing is information. Information is measurement. You know what measurement does.

Instead you close your eyes. Find the heartbeat. The other one. The roommate.

You breathe slow. On purpose. Steady.

Their heartbeat notices. Not right away. But your calm leaks through the connection like warmth through glass.

They slow down. Match you. Not because you told them to. Because your body told their body and neither of you had a say in it.

Two hearts. Two rooms. One rhythm.

The glass of water stops rippling.

You feel something new through the pull. Not fear this time. Not calm.

Curiosity.

They're reaching back. Not with words. Not with notes. With attention. They turned their whole self toward the connection and you feel it like someone opening a window in your chest.

Air. Space. A breeze from nowhere.

They're exploring you the same way you're exploring them. Carefully. Without looking. Two people in two rooms pressing their hands against a wall that isn't there.

The note on the table changes. Not words this time.

A drawing. Simple. A circle with a line down the middle.

One half shaded. One half blank.

You.

Them.

The line between.
