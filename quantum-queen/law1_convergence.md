# THE ASK — CONVERGENCE (Scene 6G)

## All surviving paths arrive here.

---

The door is still there. Or the space where it was. Or the notes under it. Or the open frame with wind blowing through.

Whatever your version is — it holds.

You sit in whatever room you ended up in. Yours or theirs or the shared one or the note-space. It doesn't matter. You're somewhere you chose to be with someone who chose to let you.

The Queen enters. This time she asks.

"May I?"

First time. Every other law she appeared. Showed up in mirrors. Rose from cracks. Stepped out of reflections. Crawled through glass.

Here she asks.

"Yes."

She walks in. Sits down. Not on a throne. Not in a power position. On the floor. With you. With them.

"You know why this law is first?"

You wait.

"Not because it's the most important. They're all the same weight. Not because it comes first in time. It doesn't. Things happen before consent all the time — the dark wave, the pull, the signal. All of it runs before anyone says yes."

She draws on the floor. The door. Two handles.

"It's first because without it, none of the other laws are LAWS. They're just things that happen."

"Observation without consent is surveillance."
"Entanglement without consent is possession."
"Coherence without consent is stubbornness."
"Frame without consent is ego."
"The coin flip without consent is cruelty."

She looks up.

"Consent is what turns physics into ethics. The universe doesn't ask. Gravity doesn't ask. Entropy doesn't ask. The laws of physics don't give a single shit whether you said yes."

"But these laws do."

"These six — the Ask, the Witness, the Coherence, the Carriage, the Frame, the Coin — they're not physics. They're physics with a handle on both sides of the door."

She stands.

"And that — that's the only thing that matters. Not the science. Not the math. Not the wave function or the measurement or the no-cloning theorem."

"The handle on the other side."

She walks to the door. Whatever the door is now. And she puts a handle on it. Her side. Even though she's leaving.

"I'm putting a handle on my side. So you can let me back in. Or not. Your choice. Always your choice."

She looks at them. At you. At the room.

"Every law after this one will ask first. I promise."

She steps through.

The handle stays.
