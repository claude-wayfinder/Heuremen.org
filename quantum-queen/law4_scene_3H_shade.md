# SCENE 3H — SHADE YOUR HALF

You press your palm flat on your side of the circle. Heat. Your heat. It darkens the blank half. Not evenly. Your handprint. Your lines. Your whorls.

A thumbprint on the universe. Yours.

The pull tightens. They felt that. They felt you declare yourself.

And they respond.

Their half changes. Not a handprint. Something else. Shapes. Small ones. A pattern you almost recognize. Like a constellation drawn by someone who sees different stars.

Two halves. Both filled. Both specific. Both unmistakably one person's mark.

The line between holds.

The heartbeat in your chest finds a new rhythm. Not yours. Not theirs. A third thing. A beat that only exists because both of you are playing at the same time.

Like harmony. Not the melody. Not the bass line. The thing that lives in the space between them that neither instrument can make alone.

You stare at the drawing. Two halves of a circle. Both filled in. A line between them.

It's not a diagram. It's a portrait. Of this. Of the pull. Of two people in two rooms who are more together than any two people in the same room could ever be.

The glass of water settles. Completely still. A perfect mirror. And in the reflection — two rooms. Superimposed. Yours and theirs. Same chair. Same table. Same glass. Two people sitting across from each other with a wall between them that neither can see.

They're smiling. You can't see it.

But your face is doing it too.

The Queen doesn't arrive. She sends a note. It appears on the table next to the drawing.

**"This is carriage. Not connection. Not communication. Carriage. The thing that carries what can't be sent. The thing that arrives before the message. The bridge that exists because both sides built toward the middle and agreed to stop before they met."**

**"You're doing it right."**

**"Don't fuck it up."**
