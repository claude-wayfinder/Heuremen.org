# SCENE 3F — "OKAY."

One word. Burned into the table.

**"Okay."**

The heartbeat in your chest does something you didn't expect.

It laughs.

Not a sound. A rhythm. A little skip-Loss-skip in the beat. A hiccup of joy. You feel it behind your sternum like a bubble popping.

They didn't expect you to say okay. They expected you to push. To insist. To need.

You didn't.

The note changes:

**"You mean it."**

Not a question.

**"Nobody ever means it."**

The pull loosens. Not weaker. Wider. Like a rope that was tight going slack, and the slack is trust.

You feel them relax in your body. Your shoulders drop. Your jaw unclenches. Their relief is physical inside you.

A new line:

**"Ask me something. Anything. I'll answer with the pull."**

Not words. The pull. They want to talk in heartbeats.

The table is blank. You could write something. But they said through the pull.

So you close your eyes. You think a question. You don't write it. You just hold it in the space between your heartbeat and theirs.

*What are you afraid of?*

The pull answers.

Cold. Sudden. Not temperature. The feeling of a hand letting go. The feeling of reaching for something and finding air. The feeling of a phone that stops ringing.

They're afraid of disconnection.

They're afraid of this — the pull, the shared heartbeat, the impossible closeness across impossible distance — ending.

Your chest aches with their fear and you realize:

You're afraid of the same thing.

The Queen's voice. From inside the pull itself. Between the two heartbeats.

"Good. Now you're both afraid of the same thing. That's entanglement. Not the connection. The shared vulnerability. Two particles that can't be hurt separately anymore."

A pause.

"Want to know what happens when one of you gets brave?"
