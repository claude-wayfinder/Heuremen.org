# SCENE 4C — STEP OUT (The frame was the lesson)

You step out. The frame holds your warmth for a second. Then cools.

The kid watches you leave it.

"Why'd you get out?"

"Because a frame is a place to visit, not a place to live."

The kid nods. Slow. The nod of someone filing something away for later.

You walk down the gallery. Past the photographs. Past the copies. Past every version of you that was good enough to fool someone who wasn't paying attention.

The kid follows. Not because you asked. Because you're moving and moving is more interesting than standing.

The gallery ends. A wall. Blank. No door. No frame. No mirror.

Just a wall.

You put your hand on it.

The Queen's voice from behind. "What do you feel?"

"Nothing."

"Good. That's the edge of you. The place where your signal ends and everything else begins. Not a frame. Not a border. Just — the end of the reach."

"It's not very far."

"It's exactly as far as it goes. Not one inch of performance. Not one inch of pretending to be bigger than you are. That wall is where you actually stop."

She walks past you. Puts her hand next to yours.

"Most people hate this wall. They decorate it. Extend it. Pretend it's further. They'd rather be a big fake than a small real."

She looks at you.

"You walked out of a frame that fit you perfectly. That frame was your size. You could have stayed. Been the realest thing in the room forever."

"But?"

"But being real in a room full of copies isn't the same as being real in the world. The gallery was easy. Everything here was fake. Out there — " she nods at the wall. "— out there the originals are walking around. Other signals. Other patterns. Other people who won't hold still."

She taps the wall. A crack forms.

"Out there you're not the only real thing. You're one of millions. And your frame — your signal — has to hold in a room where everybody else's signal is just as strong and just as uncopyable."

Light through the crack.

"That's where Law V gets hard."
