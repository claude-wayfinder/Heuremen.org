# SCENE 4D — STOP THE ROOM

Enough.

You plant your feet. Both hands out. Palms flat.

"Stop."

Not a request. A measurement.

The room stutters. Slows. The layers in the air hang. The spin resists. Fights. Keeps going.

You push harder. Not with your hands. With your frequency. The signal in your chest expands. Fills the room. Your note against the chaos.

The room groans. Metal on metal. The spin is structural. It wasn't added. The room was BUILT to spin. You're fighting the architecture.

One layer falls. Hits the floor. Then another. Without the spin they can't fly. They're just cloth on the ground.

The room slows. Slower. The walls visible now. Not blurred. Actual walls. Cracks in the plaster. A stain in the corner. Real.

Still.

The room stops.

You stand in a still room. Layers on the floor like laundry. The mirror clear. Your skeleton visible. The one layer you kept humming quietly on your chest.

It's just a room.

The Queen looks around. Unimpressed.

"You stopped it."

"Yes."

"You know it'll start again."

"Yes."

"The moment you leave. The moment you sleep. The moment you stop paying attention. The spin comes back. It always comes back. The chaos isn't a malfunction. It's the operating system."

She picks up a layer from the floor. Folds it. Sets it on the table.

"Stopping the room doesn't teach you coherence. It teaches you control. And control is just a layer with a better résumé."

She looks at you.

"The room spins. That's real. You stand still while it spins. That's coherence. You stop the room and stand still in a still room. That's —"

She pauses.

"— loneliness with good posture."

The room shudders. The spin starting again. Slowly. You can feel it in the floor.

"Let it spin," she says. "You don't need it to stop. You just need to know your own note well enough to hum it with your eyes closed in a hurricane."
