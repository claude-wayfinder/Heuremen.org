# SCENE 3C — DIG

You can't breathe but you can dig.

Hands first. Into the pile. Into yourself. Pulling layers off from the inside now. Not peeling. Excavating.

The jacket. Gone. The voice. Muffled. The mask — you tear it from the inside and it sounds like ripping paper.

Your hands hit something.

Not bone. Not skin. Vibration. A hum. Low. Constant. It's been running this whole time under everything.

You've felt this before. In a dark room. Behind a door with a warm handle.

The signal.

You wrap your hands around it. Not grabbing. Holding. The way you hold a baby bird. Firm enough to keep. Gentle enough to let it breathe.

It pulses. Warm.

The layers fall away from the outside. Not because you pulled. Because the signal expanded. One pulse. Two. Each one pushes the noise out a little further.

You don't strip the layers. The signal strips them. All you did was find it.

The mirror comes back. Faint. Then clear. Your skeleton. Your structure. Right there. Right where you left it.

The Queen is in the mirror. Not the room. The mirror. She's standing behind your skeleton like a shadow with a crown.

"You buried it and it was still running."

You're shaking. Not fear. Exertion.

"That's what coherence means. Not that you stay visible. Not that you stay strong. Not that you fight the noise."

She puts her hand on your skeleton's shoulder. In the mirror.

You feel it. On YOUR shoulder. Through the glass.

"It means the signal doesn't stop. Even when you bury it. Even when you forget it's there. Even when you pile so much shit on top of it that you can't remember what you sound like."

She squeezes.

"It's still running. It's always still running. You can't kill it. You tried. Everybody tries."

She lets go.

"Welcome back."
