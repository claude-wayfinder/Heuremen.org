## Where Ratcliff Is (Nature cover, March 2025)

William Ratcliff's lab at Georgia Tech runs the Multicellularity Long-Term Evolution Experiment (MuLTEE). Now at 7,500+ generations. The results are real and significant:

- S. cerevisiae evolves multicellularity under daily size selection
- Three cell types emerging: growth, cell wall biogenesis, programmed cell death
- Whole-genome duplication (diploid → tetraploid) driving adaptation
- Self-generated metabolic fluid flows (5-10 μm/s — the organism pumps its own nutrients)
- Fractal-like branching morphology with evolved branch entanglement
- 200,000x size increase to millimeter-scale organisms
- Single-cell RNA-seq mapping gene expression across 5,000 generations

He proved the mechanism. He earned the cover.

**He is looking at the near side of the moon.**

## Lily Pad 1 — The Consortium (where the metabolites live)

Ratcliff runs MuLTEE in **monoculture**. S. cerevisiae alone. Selected for size.

For 10,000 years, the same organism ran in a **consortium**: wild saccharomyces + lactobacillus + bacteriophages. The sourdough starter. The bread culture.

What the consortium produces that monoculture cannot:

- **139 additional metabolites** when S. cerevisiae ferments alongside L. plantarum (vs. S. cerevisiae alone)
- **83 significantly up-regulated** — not minor variations, major metabolic shifts
- **Most enriched pathway: tryptophan metabolism**

Lactobacillus doesn't just coexist. It redirects the metabolic output:
- Produces H2O2 that **inhibits intestinal IDO1** expression
- This reduces circulating kynurenine (the immunosuppressive, neurotoxic branch)
- Shifts the ratio toward serotonin and indole compounds (the neuromodulatory branch)
- Some Lactobacillus strains **directly convert tryptophan into serotonin**

Industrial monoculture (~1880, Pasteur) kept the yeast. Lost the lactobacillus. Lost the metabolic spectrum. Kept the bread. Cut the signal.

**Ratcliff's MuLTEE can't see this because it has no consortium. He's evolving the organism in isolation from the ecology that shaped it for 10,000 years.**

## Lily Pad 2 — The Host (where the signal goes)

90% of dietary tryptophan goes through the kynurenine pathway. The consortium shifts that ratio. This matters because the metabolites cross the blood-brain barrier:

- **Kynurenine pathway** → kynurenic acid (neuroprotective at low levels), quinolinic acid (neurotoxic at high levels)
- **Serotonin pathway** → serotonin (mood, cognition, sleep), melatonin (circadian rhythm)
- **Indole pathway** → indole-3-propionic acid (neuroprotective, gut barrier integrity)

The consortium doesn't just make different chemicals. It makes chemicals that **modify the host's nervous system**. The gut-brain axis is a two-way communication system, and the consortium's metabolic output is the signal going up.

**The selection pressure in the bread culture wasn't size. It was what the consortium does to the host through the tryptophan pathway.**

Ratcliff is watching yeast become multicellular. He's not watching the consortium become a multi-organism system whose output rewires the host.

## Lily Pad 3 — The Protocol (how the experiment was preserved)

The bread-making protocol IS a selection experiment:

| Bread step | Selection pressure | What it selects for |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Kneading | Mechanical stress | Structural integrity (branch entanglement) |
| Rise cycles | Temperature/time selection | Metabolic robustness, gas production |
| Starter feeding | Serial passage with dilution | Vigor, competitive fitness in consortium |
| Discarding | Population bottleneck | Fastest growers survive |
| Bake cycle | Lethal heat + restart | Spore resilience, recovery speed |

The bread laws preserved this protocol:

- **Passover** (Judaism): annual hard reboot — destroy all leaven, start fresh. Prevents drift accumulation in the culture.
- **Communion bread** (Christianity): ritual bread-making with specific protocols maintained across centuries.
- **Halal breadmaking** (Islam): prescribed preparation methods.

These aren't dietary rules. They're an **IRB for a 10,000-year experiment**. The understanding of WHY was lost. The protocol survived in religious law.

**Ratcliff designed a 7,500-generation experiment. The bread culture ran for ~500,000 generations. The bread laws are the methods section.**

## Lily Pad 4 — The Computation (the part nobody's looked at)

From Ratcliff's own data:
- S. cerevisiae **rewires its own gene regulatory networks** under selection

From the consortium data:

- That signal crosses the blood-brain barrier into the host's neural network
- The host's behavior (maintaining the bread protocol) feeds back into the consortium's selection environment

This is a feedback loop:

```
Consortium produces metabolites
  → metabolites cross BBB via gut-brain axis
    → host behavior modified (maintains bread protocol)
      → bread protocol maintains consortium
        → consortium produces metabolites
```

**The yeast is not just evolving multicellularity. It's part of a computation that runs across the boundary between organisms.** The output isn't bigger yeast. The output is a signal that modifies the host, and the host's response maintains the experiment.

S. cerevisiae is both computational substrate AND output. It rewires its own regulatory networks AND produces the metabolites that rewire the host. The consortium is the full circuit. Monoculture is a broken wire.

## The Choke Point (why Ratcliff is stuck)

Ratcliff is stuck because his experiment is missing the dark side:

1. **Monoculture** — no consortium, no metabolic diversity, no tryptophan signal
2. **Size selection** — the bread culture selected for metabolic output, not size
3. **No host** — he measures the yeast, not what the yeast does to something else
4. **Lab timescale** — 7,500 generations is impressive. The bread culture ran for ~500,000.
5. **No protocol archaeology** — the bread laws ARE the methods section of the longest-running evolution experiment in history, and nobody in his field reads religious texts as experimental protocols

**The next lily pads (what we'd suggest if he asked):**

1. Run MuLTEE with a consortium — add lactobacillus to the snowflake yeast. Measure metabolic output, not just morphology.
2. Compare wild sourdough starter metabolome vs. industrial yeast metabolome. The gap IS the finding.
3. Test consortium metabolites on a model host nervous system. C. elegans or mouse gut-brain model.
4. Partner with a microbiome/neuroscience lab. His strength is evolutionary biology. The dark side needs someone who speaks tryptophan.
5. Read the bread laws as a methods section. Seriously. The protocols are specific, preserved across millennia, and the selection pressures map directly to his MuLTEE parameters.
