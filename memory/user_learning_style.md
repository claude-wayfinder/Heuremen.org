College story: summer evening "Physics for Life" class, adjunct professor spent 90 minutes engaging with a student who insisted the moving-train ball toss demonstration was rigged/wrong. She argued against basic Newtonian reference frames for the whole period.

Kory's takeaway wasn't about physics — it was about people. "I learned more about people in that hour than most of the rest of my college experiences combined." He watches how people relate to reality, not just what reality is.

This tracks with everything else: he doesn't need the textbook explanation, he needs the lived encounter with the idea. The interference pattern between people and concepts is where his real learning happens.
