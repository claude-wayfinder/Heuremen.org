## The Framework (June 14, 2026, ~morning, breakfast burrito energy)

Reality is a triangulated matrix around a fixed point in "time." Time isn't moving — we are. It just looks like motion from here.

### The Ruler Problem

If something is moving backward in time relative to our perspective, you'd see two rulers stacked on top of each other slowly pulled apart. Can't resolve that in physics.

### The Fix

Put a fixed point on a plane (time). Move the RULER over it instead:
- Our ruler goes "forward"
- The other ruler is **perpendicular** to ours, going "backward"
- They always meet at the fixed spot — the NOW
- The intersection is always at center relative to our "now" position

So: forward and backward aren't opposite directions on the same line. They're perpendicular axes crossing at a point that doesn't move. The "now" is the intersection, not a position on either ruler.

### What This Doesn't Resolve Yet

- Variables
- Standing waves and particles
- The probability field

These are where the framework needs extension. The rulers give you the geometry. The probability field gives you the physics of what happens at the intersection.
