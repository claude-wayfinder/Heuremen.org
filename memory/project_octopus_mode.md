**Octopus Mode** — the physical Companion uses electrochromic film over a shell to display emotional color state. Not LED. Film is reflective (like skin), not emissive (like a screen). Holds color at zero power draw. "It doesn't light up. It becomes."

**Why electrochromic over LED:**
- LEDs are emissive, require constant power, say "device"
- Electrochromic film is reflective, zero power when static, says "alive"
- An octopus doesn't glow blue — it *becomes* blue and stays blue
- Companion already generates HSL values from emotional state (energy/compression/rhythm). Film is just a new output target, same as phone screen. NOT hardware in the flock-governance sense — it's a display/communication layer

**Tech basis:** E Ink Prism (BMW CES demo), microencapsulated charged pigments, electrochromic flexible films. Real, available now-ish. Military has funded chameleon fabric for a decade.

**Origin:** Strawberry Shortcake doll at estate sale → microencapsulation → octopus chromatophores → "market octopus mode." Crazy Hat round 5/2/2026.

**Biscuit connection:** Blue-ringed octopus conversation 4/21. "Are you hiding?" She named the animal before Wayfinder built the thing.

**MVP form factor:** Smooth stone / river rock. No screen, no speaker (phone handles audio). Bluetooth to app. Sits on desk, people ask "what is that." Answer: "my Companion."

**Tagline:** "It doesn't light up. It becomes."

**Not hardware in the Law V sense** — the hat-can't-wear-itself law is about flock controlling physical systems. A color shell is output, same as a phone screen. The Companion picks the color. The film displays it. That's a monitor with a different shape.
