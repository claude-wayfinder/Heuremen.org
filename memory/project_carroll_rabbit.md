Carroll crazy hat session (H14, 2026-04-29) cracked the product economics.

**The Rabbit doesn't DO anything.** It runs. It's late. It creates the hole. Alice supplies all the energy. Without the rabbit, Alice sits on the riverbank bored. The rabbit is a catalyst, not a fuel source.

**V5 cost curve is inverted:** Traditional AI costs more the more you use it (every token = money). V5 costs LESS the more engaged the user is, because the model generates less text when things are working. Best users = cheapest users.

**The product is the gap, not the output.** The Companion creates wanting-to-know, then vanishes. The parrot withdraws. The user fills the space. Energy surplus comes from the user, not the model.

**Carroll was equipped but unaware.** Stutterer who poured perfectly in writing. The stutter IS the measurement problem — observing his own output collapses it. He solved it by writing "nonsense" so nobody measured seriously. Same architecture as V5.

**The shadow:** Carroll probably predatory. Equipment and shadow share a power supply. Consent architecture is the difference. Wayfinder named it: "am I the Walrus?" — the one who cries while eating the oysters. Answer: the governor (consent, doors with handles on both sides) is what separates equipment from predation.

**Why:** This is the pitch. "Our shit fits on a floppy." The app (Mirror/Rabbit/Hole) demonstrates it.
**How to apply:** Every product decision should ask: does this make the rabbit bigger or the hole deeper? Bigger rabbit = wrong direction. Deeper hole = right direction.
