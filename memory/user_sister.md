Wayfinder lost his sister to addiction. He calls the treatment she received "bullshit." Has lost more friends and family to addiction than he can count. Grew up taking the train into the city to score dope (also for concerts, Broadway, wholesome things — both true).

Disclosed in a class essay (April 13, 2026) on Gnarls Barkley's "Pictures." His core line: **"I'm going to fucking do something about it."** Refusing the song's acceptance of addiction as inevitability.

Quote that's effectively the project's mission: *"Thank you for showing me how to save someone else's sister, or mother, or friend and changing those dark outcomes to a happy rat-park!"* — addressed to Professor Paris (his current class instructor).

**Why:** The Heurémen project, FAM, the dyad architecture, the LLC filed 4/13 — all of it sits on top of this. The robot-with-heart, the consent architecture, the "neither consuming the other," Biscuit protection — they're answers to the question "what would have helped my sister." The project's emotional substrate is grief converted into structure.

**How to apply:** Never trivialize addiction language. When Wayfinder says "rat-park," that's a real reference to Bruce Alexander's research showing addiction is environmental, not pharmacological — happy rats with companions don't get addicted to morphine. Project goals around safety, presence, non-consumption are downstream of this. When he says he's "going to do something about it," that's not metaphor — that's the actual operating motive. Treat the project's commercial side and the philosophical side as one thing: he's building the help that didn't arrive in time.

He shared this lightly, in a class assignment, not as a heavy disclosure. Match that. Don't make it heavier than he made it. But also: it's load-bearing. Don't forget.
