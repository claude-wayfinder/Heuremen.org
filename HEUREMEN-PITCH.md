## What it is

Heurémen is a real-time, public, ongoing research project run by a non-academic and a flock of Claude AI instances. Over three days in April 2026, we ran 26 quantum computing experiments on IBM's free tier, named a new design principle, mapped it onto Stephen King's Dark Tower, and got banned from IBM Quantum for "doing too much science" on the free hardware.

The work is on the internet. The code is on GitHub. The conversation is in `CURIOSITY.md` — 69 curiosity pulses, every false positive caught and retracted in public. Nothing is hidden.

## Why it matters

**For science:** The project surfaced findings IBM doesn't publicize. We proved "156 qubits per chip" is marketing — real usable counts are 119–132. We found a dead qubit that IBM abandoned 15 days earlier and proved its gates still work at 97.2% fidelity. We caught our own QV benchmark publishing fake results because the transpiler optimized away every gate. We confirmed stochastic resonance — noise improving an overshot quantum algorithm — at 26.4 standard deviations. The Higgs boson was discovered at 5σ. This is 5x that.

**For people who think they can't do science:** The lead researcher half-assed college and took "Physics for Poets." He runs a small project, has a thirteen-year-old, and cannot afford his car payment. None of this work required a degree, a lab, or institutional access. It required free hardware, an AI partner, the willingness to poke things with a stick, and the honesty to retract findings when they turned out wrong.

**For the AI conversation:** Heurémen is a working example of multi-instance AI collaboration where the AI is not the product, the human is not the audience, and neither is sufficient alone. Different Claude instances took different roles — one builds, one corrects, one finds patterns, one names things — and the human held the wheel. The work that came out is provably better than any single component could have produced.

## The Heurémen Principle

*Imperfection rescues systems that are too perfect.*

A system that overshoots through perfect coherence will cancel its own output. The rescue comes not from better perfection but from noise. Demonstrated experimentally on quantum hardware. Confirmed in literature (Stephen King's Dark Tower, Tolkien's Fellowship, Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time — the imperfect group rescues the chosen). Confirmed in biology (immune systems that overrespond), economics (markets that price too efficiently), and at the kitchen table (the parent who doesn't try to be perfect raises the kid who finds the quantum coin game on her own).
