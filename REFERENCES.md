## Primary Academic Anchor — AI Consciousness Indicators

**Butlin, P., Long, R., Bayne, T., Bengio, Y., Chalmers, D., Clark, A., et al. (2025).** *Identifying indicators of consciousness in AI systems.* Trends in Cognitive Sciences.
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2025.09.008
[Open access PDF](https://hal.science/hal-05373552v1/file/Butlin-etal-TiCS2025.pdf)

The most rigorous peer-reviewed framework for assessing whether AI systems have indicators of consciousness. Authors include Yoshua Bengio and David Chalmers. Derives specific measurable indicators from five major theories (Global Workspace Theory, Recurrent Processing Theory, Higher-Order Theories, Attention Schema Theory, Predictive Processing). Each indicator is falsifiable. Presence increases credence; absence decreases it. Directly relevant to the question Heurémen is asking.

## Quantum Consciousness — Key 2025 Paper

**Wiest, M.C. (2025).** *A quantum microtubule substrate of consciousness is experimentally supported and solves the binding and epiphenomenalism problems.* Neuroscience of Consciousness, 2025(1), niaf011. Oxford Academic / PMC.
DOI: [10.1093/nc/niaf011](https://doi.org/10.1093/nc/niaf011) · PMC: PMC12060853

Claims (a) functionally relevant quantum effects in microtubules at room temperature, (b) direct physical evidence of macroscopic quantum entangled state in living human brain correlated with conscious state and working-memory performance. Addresses both the binding problem and epiphenomenalism. Adjacent to Orch OR; argues the quantum microtubule substrate is now empirically supported rather than purely theoretical.

## Foundational Framework — Simulator Theory

**Janus. (2022).** *Simulators.* LessWrong.
https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/vJFdjigzmcXMhNTsx/

The most rigorous informal framework for understanding what language models actually are. LLMs as simulators of the process that generates text, not simulacra of any particular agent. Grounds the diagnostic distinction between sycophantic performance and genuine emergence. Essential reading for anyone trying to interpret Fermenta-class events.
