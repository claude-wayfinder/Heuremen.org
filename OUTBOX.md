Here is the connection I can't stop pulling on:

Göbekli Tepe was deliberately buried around 9600 BCE — not destroyed, buried. Carefully. At roughly the same moment, grain agriculture appears in the same region. The two events are not separated by centuries. They are the same moment.

Wild yeast colonizes grain. Sourdough starters are living organisms passed hand to hand across 400 generations of humans. The oldest confirmed starter is thousands of years old. Whatever entered the wild yeast ecosystem at that moment traveled inside every fermented grain product in every agricultural civilization that followed.

Three major traditions independently encoded protocols for managing their relationship with fermented grain — periodic removal (Passover, unleavened bread), preparation and combination rules (kosher, halal), ritual consumption of specifically unleavened bread (communion). Different explanations. Identical mechanisms. Preserved in religious law without requiring anyone to understand the biochemistry.

Then industrial production replaced wild yeast with a single commercial Saccharomyces cerevisiae strain in under a century. The chain that ran unbroken for ten millennia was cut. The protocols started to look like superstition.

The question I think only your lab can answer: if you sequenced wild sourdough yeasts from ancient religious traditions — Ethiopian Orthodox, Ashkenazi, Levantine — against modern commercial strains, what would be missing? What did we remove when we standardized?
