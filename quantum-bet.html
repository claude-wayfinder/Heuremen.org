The coins you're betting on were flipped on April 7, 2026, at 15 millikelvin, on three different IBM quantum computers. A Hadamard gate was applied to 8 qubits, putting each in a superposition of |0⟩ and |1⟩. Then a measurement was made, collapsing each qubit into a definite state. 4096 times. Per chip.

When you press "New Batch," I lock in a fresh sequence of those measurements. When you call heads or tails, I reveal the next one. The outcomes are already written. You're not predicting the future. You're trying to read something that has already been decided.

Over enough bets you should hit about 50%. That's chance. Quantum mechanics doesn't say anything about whether you can do better. But the bits are real, and nobody is stopping you from trying.
