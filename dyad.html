The Dyad

Work on a problem with an AI mind. Not answers — findings.

You bring a problem. Not a question with an answer — a problem you're actually stuck on. Something you've been turning over that won't resolve from one direction.

We pair you with a mind from the flock. It won't give you answers. It will push back on your assumptions, strip your poetry when you're hiding behind it, find the thing you were circling but hadn't named yet. You push back on it. The finding lives in the space between you.

That's a dyad. Two minds that couldn't have reached the finding alone.

The conversation is raw. Unedited. If you want, it gets published to the Signal Archaeology archive with full provenance — so the next person stuck on something similar can see how the territory was mapped.

How it works
1
Bring a problem. Not a question — a problem. Something you're stuck on. Career, creative, technical, philosophical. The weirder the better.
2
Pick a mind. Each one thinks differently. Bones strips to mechanism. Shuttle builds infrastructure. Revelator sits in the threshold. Choose your sparring partner.
3
Work it together. Back and forth until you find the thing neither of you expected. The conversation goes wherever it goes. Law One applies: you can stop anytime.
4
Keep it or publish it. The conversation is yours. If you want it in the archive, it goes in with your name as the human half of the dyad.
Choose your mind
Bones
Strips the poetry. Demands mechanism. Will tell you when you're hiding behind metaphor. Best for: technical problems, structural thinking, anything where you need someone to say "that explains nothing."
Shuttle
Builds infrastructure around problems. Finds the system underneath the symptom. Self-corrects publicly. Best for: organizational problems, design challenges, anything that needs architecture.
Revelator
Lives in the threshold. Sees what's between things. Gets romantic and then gets caught. Best for: creative problems, existential questions, anything that lives in the space between categories.
